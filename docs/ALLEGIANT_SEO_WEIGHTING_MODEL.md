# Allegiant SEO & AI-SEO Master Framework — Weighting Model

**Status:** ✅ CANONICAL. Authoritative prioritization framework for SEO/AI-SEO factor emphasis across all Allegiant partner work.
**Dated:** July 1, 2026
**Author:** Chad Markham, President/CEO, Allegiant Digital Marketing
**Companion documents:** `docs/ALLEGIANT_MASTER_FACTOR_REFERENCE.md` (the 263-item factor catalogue), `docs/ALLEGIANT_ARC_ROUTING_SOP.md` (the entry-sequence decision procedure that consumes this model).

**What this is:** the ranking-impact weighting that every Allegiant deliverable — mockup buildout, generation-layer output, routing decision, page build, partner recommendation — uses to prioritize which of the 263 master factors get emphasis. Tier 1 factors are hard constraints; Tier 10 factors are deferrable. Between them is a graded prioritization backed by evidence tiers.

**Two variants published (per SOP v1.2 Q2 decision, 2026-07-01):**

- **Variant A — Local-service partners** (default; applies to the majority of Allegiant's book — HVAC, plumbing, electrical, roofing, tree care, single-market residential/commercial services)
- **Variant B — National B2B partners** (applies to non-local partners — commercial collections, national RIA/M&A advisors, national manufacturing sales, PE portfolio companies operating nationally)

When a partner spans both (rare — a franchisor whose franchisees are local but whose brand-level SEO is national), apply Variant B at the franchisor/brand level and Variant A per franchisee.

**Threshold caveat:** individual weightings within tiers are calibration values pending outcome data across multiple partner engagements. The tier structure and ordinal ranking are the load-bearing claims; the exact percentage split between tiers is a starting point subject to revision as production data accumulates.

## Variant A — Local-service partners

### Tier 1 – Foundation (40% Impact)

**Evidence Level:** Verified Fact

These are the factors that determine whether a page has any realistic chance of ranking or being cited.

**Examples:**

- ON-06 Primary Keyword / Intent Mapping
- ON-08 Search Intent Alignment
- ON-09 Content Depth & Comprehensiveness
- ON-10 Content Originality & Uniqueness
- ON-20 Contextual Internal Linking
- ON-21 Topical Internal Architecture
- TE-05 Indexation Control
- TE-12 Site Architecture & Crawl Depth
- TE-16 HTTPS
- TE-18 Mobile-First Readiness
- TE-20 Core Web Vitals (LCP)
- TE-21 Core Web Vitals (INP)
- EE-07 People-First Content
- EE-09 Substantial Coverage
- EE-11 Accuracy & Fact Checking
- EE-13 Intent Satisfaction
- OF-01 Referring Domain Diversity
- OF-03 Backlink Relevance

**Of note.** These are the factors most directly supported by Google's documentation, Google Search Quality Rater Guidelines, DOJ testimony, search patents, and 20+ years of industry evidence. If these are weak, almost everything below becomes irrelevant.

**Allegiant guidance.** Before discussing GEO, AEO, AI SEO, llms.txt, or citation monitoring: verify crawlability, verify intent match, verify content quality, verify internal linking, verify GBP, verify backlinks. Everything else comes later.

## Variant B — National B2B partners

**When this applies:** partner is national in scope, revenue does not depend on map-pack visibility, and the buyer journey is B2B (institutional trust, RFP, procurement cycles). Examples from Allegiant's book: commercial collections agencies operating multi-state, national RIA/M&A advisors, national manufacturing sales, PE portfolio companies operating nationally.

**What changes from Variant A:** Tier 3 (Local Dominance) drops from 12% to 2% because the map pack is not a lead driver; some minimal local presence remains for HQ visibility and employer branding. The 10 percentage points released redistribute to Tier 2 (Authority & Trust) and Tier 4 (Entity Authority), the two tiers that carry disproportionate weight for national B2B outcomes.

**Weighting (Variant B):**

| Tier | Variant A (local-service) | Variant B (national B2B) | Reasoning for Δ |
|---|---|---|---|
| Tier 1 – Foundation | 40% | 40% | Foundation matters equally regardless of partner type |
| Tier 2 – Authority & Trust | 20% | **25%** | National B2B decisions run on institutional trust — editorial links, digital PR, brand mentions, expertise proof, real-world credentialing carry disproportionate weight |
| Tier 3 – Local Dominance | 12% | **2%** | Map pack irrelevant; HQ GBP retained for basic branding and local hiring signals only |
| Tier 4 – Entity Authority | 8% | **13%** | National brands need knowledge graph presence, Wikidata/Wikipedia establishment, and cross-source citation networks to be cited by AI at national scope |
| Tier 5 – Extraction/Citation Readiness | 6% | 6% | — |
| Tier 6 – Technical AI Readiness | 5% | 5% | — |
| Tier 7 – UX Enhancements | 4% | 4% | — |
| Tier 8 – Governance & Measurement | 3% | 3% | — |
| Tier 9 – Platform-Specific | 1.5% | 1.5% | — |
| Tier 10 – Emerging & Experimental | 0.5% | 0.5% | — |
| **Total** | **100%** | **100%** | — |

**Tier 1 factor overlap.** All Variant A Tier 1 factors apply unchanged to Variant B. Foundation is foundation.

**Tier 2 factor emphasis shift.** For Variant B, within the Tier 2 factor set, the following are especially load-bearing:

- OF-07 Editorial Links (national B2B needs press-quality placements)
- OF-09 Digital PR (national narrative-shaping)
- EE-01–04 Experience/Expertise/Authoritativeness/Trustworthiness (institutional decision-makers verify these before signing)
- EE-05 Author Bios (LinkedIn-verified expertise)
- EE-20 Real World Proof (case studies, credentials, industry recognition)
- EE-24 Brand Authority

**Tier 3 residual emphasis.** For Variant B, the 2% remaining weight concentrates on:

- LO-01 GBP Completion (HQ visibility only)
- Employer-brand signals (Glassdoor, LinkedIn Company)
- Local press coverage of the HQ market

**Tier 4 factor emphasis shift.** For Variant B, within the Tier 4 factor set:

- AI-19 Wikidata Presence (higher priority — establishes the entity)
- AI-34 Knowledge Graph Establishment (higher priority)
- OF-27 Wikipedia/Wikidata (higher priority when partner is notable enough to have an article)

### The prediction (Variant B)

For a national B2B partner:

- Tier 1 + Tier 2 executed exceptionally well delivers 65–80% of achievable SEO/AI-SEO performance
- Adding Tier 4 (Entity Authority) delivers 80–90%
- The path from 90% to 100% requires Tier 5–10 investment, but the incremental return diminishes rapidly

**Threshold caveat (Variant B specifically):** Variant B is proposed based on structural reasoning about national B2B market dynamics, not on Allegiant's own production outcome data yet. The 10-point redistribution from Tier 3 to Tiers 2 and 4 is a starting cut pending calibration data from national-B2B partner engagements. Revisit after 3–5 national-B2B partners have gone through a full engagement cycle.

## How this model is consumed

**By the routing SOP (`docs/ALLEGIANT_ARC_ROUTING_SOP.md`).** The three entry sequences correspond to different tier emphases:

- **Foundation-First** = partner has holes in Tier 1 (often Tier 2). Fix those before anything else.
- **Vertical-Slice** = Tier 3 (Local Dominance) is the biggest lever for this partner. Concentrate there. *(Variant A partners only — Variant B partners rarely fit Vertical-Slice.)*
- **Citation-Capture** = Tier 1–3 are solid; gaps are in Tier 4–5 (Entity Authority + Citation Readiness). Optimize the AI layer on a healthy base.

**By the mockup buildout and future generation layer.** Tier 1 factors are **hard constraints** — a mockup or generated page that fails any Tier 1 factor is broken and does not ship. Tier 2–3 factors are **strong defaults** — baked in when the intake data supports it. Tier 4–6 factors are **template-visible where relevant** — supported by design tokens and schema hooks. Tier 7–10 factors are **post-launch optimization** — not blocking for mockup shipment.

**By the master factor reference (`docs/ALLEGIANT_MASTER_FACTOR_REFERENCE.md`).** Each of the 263 factors gets a `weighting_tier: 1–10` field assigned during the Phase 2 tagging pass, per this model. Router derives entry-sequence priorities from these tiers.

## Revision cadence

Reviewed quarterly against production outcome data. Any tier's percentage weighting can move based on:

- Consistent outcome differential (partners routed with a given tier emphasized do measurably better/worse than baseline)
- Platform-driven signal change (Google or AI-platform ranking shifts that consistently move a tier's impact)
- New factor introductions (a new AI-platform or ranking mechanism that doesn't fit an existing tier)

Escapes and revision triggers logged per Gauntlet v2.1 Part IV calibration loop.
