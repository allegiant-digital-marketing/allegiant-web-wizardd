# Allegiant Digital — Master SEO & AI-SEO Factor Reference
*The consolidated, fully-specified factor list across SEO, AI SEO/GEO/AEO, Technical, UX, E-E-A-T, Local, Platform-Specific, and Measurement & Governance. 263 factors across 9 parts. Compiled June 2026.*

This is the agency's single source of truth: every ranking, citation, experience, and governance factor that determines whether a page wins organic position **and** gets cited by AI answer engines — each one specified to a uniform, actionable template.

---

## How each factor is specified
Every factor uses the same seven fields:
**Definition** · **Why it matters (+ engines)** · **Implement** · **Acceptance** (binary where possible) · **Tooling** · **Failure modes** · **Verify.**
"Engines" names which surfaces the factor moves: Google/Bing organic, Google AI Overviews/AI Mode, LLM citation (ChatGPT/Claude/Perplexity/Gemini/Copilot), and rich results.

## Reading conventions
- **ID scheme** (the prefix tells you the part): `ON` On-Page · `OF` Off-Page · `TE` Technical · `UX` UX & Page Experience · `EE` E-E-A-T · `LO` Local · `AI` AI SEO/GEO/AEO · `PF` Platform-Specific · `ME` Measurement & Governance.
- **Cross-references** appear as bare IDs in the text (e.g. "see TE-29," "ties EE-19"). Use the **[Index](#index--all-263-factors)** to jump — every part and every factor has a stable anchor.
- **Acceptance checks are binary** wherever the factor allows, so a page either passes or it doesn't.

## OMNIVIZ™ mapping
The AI layer (Parts 7–8, and the AI-monitoring items in Part 9) is the operational expansion of the OMNIVIZ framework. Each of those factors carries a **pillar tag**:
- **EAB — Entity Authority Building:** being a recognized, consistently-identified entity (entities, topical authority, brand demand, Knowledge Graph).
- **ACA — Answer-First Content Architecture:** structure AI can lift cleanly (answer-first, definition-first, self-contained chunks, semantic completeness, Q&A, tables).
- **MCN — Multi-Source Citation Network:** the off-site corroboration AI weighs (mentions, cross-platform presence, community/Reddit, third-party validation).
- **TAR — Technical AI Readiness:** machine-accessibility (raw-HTML content, schema, AI-crawler access, llms.txt).
- **AVM — AI Visibility Monitoring:** measuring citation, share-of-voice, and AI referral per platform.

The four disciplines those pillars serve: **AEO** (Answer Engine Optimization), **GEO** (Generative Engine Optimization), **AI SEO**, and **LLM SEO**.

## Allegiant standards baked in
- **"Partners," never "clients"** — on every surface.
- **No fabrication, ever** — no invented stats, claims, case studies, or results; every corrected claim logs **Before/After/Source**; external claims carry a verified outbound link to the issuing authority. This standard extends to **reporting itself** (ME-13/15).
- **Pflugerville is Chad's private residence — it never appears** in public copy, schema, GBP, or citations. Body copy uses **"Austin TX"**; the full street address is used only on NAP-critical surfaces (Org/LocalBusiness schema, contact, GBP).
- **Byline standard:** Chad Markham + Mike Small (Dir. Production) for SEO/AI SEO pages (add the current SEO lead once designated); Chad Markham + Mike Small + Will Holt (SEM Manager) for SEM pages — each with a real bio and Person schema.
- **Governance = the Gauntlet** (P1–P5, binary, the four hostile reads never collapsed, Tier-1 = human peer review); a deliverable is **Complete only after sign-off**.
- **Live-only hyperlinking**, the standard 5-block head schema (Organization / Service / Person / FAQ / Breadcrumb), and html5lib-clean markup apply throughout.

## Cross-cutting honesty principles
These calibrations run end-to-end and are what keep the reference defensible:
- **The AI layer sits *on top of* the foundation.** Parts 1–6 build the technically-sound, authoritative, well-structured site that AI retrieves from; Parts 7–8 make it citable. AI work does not replace the fundamentals.
- **Behavioral signals are evidence-based, not confirmed.** Dwell, pogo-sticking, and CTR (UX-11/12/13) are framed on the Navboost/API-leak/DOJ-testimony evidence — optimize the underlying satisfaction, not the metric.
- **llms.txt is emerging, not established** (AI-11/12): low-cost, potential-upside, unconfirmed adoption — never treated as a guaranteed lever, and the fabricated vendor "directives" are excluded.
- **Platform mechanics are observed and volatile** (Part 8): index dependencies (e.g. ChatGPT→Bing, Claude→Brave) are reported, not contractual, and can change — PF-25 is the standing process to keep them current.
- **Measure outcomes, not vanity** (Part 9): leads and revenue over rankings and traffic; and **attribution is inherently imperfect** — model it, don't over-claim precision.

<a id="part1"></a>
## Part 1 · On-Page / On-Site (ON-01 – ON-35)

**Template per item:** Definition · Why it matters (+ engines) · Implement · Acceptance · Tooling · Failure modes · Verify.
**Conventions:** Acceptance criteria are written as binary checks wherever possible (Gauntlet discipline). No fabricated statistics — claims are qualitative or well-established. "Engines" notes which surfaces the factor moves: Google/Bing organic, AI Overviews/AI Mode, LLM citation (ChatGPT/Claude/Perplexity/Gemini/Copilot), or CTR.

---

<a id="ON-01"></a>
### ON-01 · Title tag optimization
- **Definition.** The `<title>` element — the page's clickable SERP headline and the strongest single on-page relevance signal tied to one URL.
- **Why / engines.** Primary relevance + CTR lever (Google/Bing organic); used as the citation label by AI Overviews and LLMs.
- **Implement.** One unique title per page; lead with the primary term, append the brand; keep to ~50–60 characters / ~600px to avoid truncation; write for the click and the intent, not just the keyword.
- **Acceptance.** Unique site-wide · primary term present · renders untruncated at 600px · no stuffing.
- **Tooling.** Screaming Frog (length/duplicate audit), Search Console (query/CTR), a SERP-preview tool.
- **Failure modes.** Duplicated titles across templated/location pages; brand-first burying the term; truncation; keyword stuffing; Google rewriting a weak title.
- **Verify.** Crawl for duplicate/over-length titles; eyeball live SERP rendering for the 5 top money pages.

<a id="ON-02"></a>
### ON-02 · Meta description optimization
- **Definition.** The `<meta name="description">` snippet shown beneath the title in SERPs.
- **Why / engines.** Not a direct ranking factor; a CTR lever (Google/Bing). Google rewrites it ~60%+ of the time, so treat it as influence, not control.
- **Implement.** ~150–160 characters; front-load the value proposition; include the primary term naturally (it bolds on match); end with an implicit CTA; unique per page.
- **Acceptance.** Unique · ≤ ~160 chars · primary term present · reads as a benefit, not a keyword list.
- **Tooling.** Screaming Frog, Search Console (CTR by query), SERP preview.
- **Failure modes.** Missing on key pages; duplicated across templates; truncated; generic boilerplate.
- **Verify.** Crawl for missing/duplicate descriptions; compare CTR before/after a rewrite on a sample of pages.

<a id="ON-03"></a>
### ON-03 · URL slug structure
- **Definition.** The human-readable path segment identifying the page.
- **Why / engines.** Minor ranking signal; meaningful for UX, click confidence, and clean citation/share appearance (all engines).
- **Implement.** Short, descriptive, hyphen-separated, lowercase; include the primary term; drop stop-words and dates; keep stable (changing a URL forces a redirect).
- **Acceptance.** Lowercase · hyphenated · ≤ ~5 words · term present · no parameters on indexable pages · stable.
- **Tooling.** Screaming Frog (URL audit), CMS URL settings.
- **Failure modes.** Auto-generated gibberish/IDs; underscores; deep nesting; changing URLs without 301s; dates that age the page.
- **Verify.** Crawl URL patterns for length/case/parameters; confirm any changed URL 301-redirects.

<a id="ON-04"></a>
### ON-04 · H1 tag
- **Definition.** The page's primary on-page heading.
- **Why / engines.** Reinforces topic/intent to crawlers and to readers landing from search (Google/Bing; supports AI extraction by labeling the main subject).
- **Implement.** One clear H1 per page, intent-aligned; distinct from (not identical to) the title tag; describes what the page delivers.
- **Acceptance.** Exactly one H1 · aligned to primary intent · not a verbatim copy of the title.
- **Tooling.** Screaming Frog (H1 count/duplication), browser inspector.
- **Failure modes.** Multiple H1s from theme templates; H1 = title duplicate; H1 styled but generic ("Welcome"); missing H1.
- **Verify.** Crawl for pages with 0 or 2+ H1s; spot-check that the H1 answers "what is this page about."

<a id="ON-05"></a>
### ON-05 · Heading hierarchy
- **Definition.** The logical nesting of H2–H6 that structures the page's content.
- **Why / engines.** Drives scannability and machine parsing; well-formed sub-headings map to the sub-questions AI engines fan a query into (Google/Bing; AI Overviews; LLM extraction).
- **Implement.** Nest logically (no skipping levels for styling); phrase H2s as the questions/subtopics users actually search; one idea per section.
- **Acceptance.** No skipped levels · subheads cover the topic's sub-questions · each section is self-contained.
- **Tooling.** Screaming Frog, headingsMap browser extension, the page outline view.
- **Failure modes.** Headings chosen for visual size; vague labels ("More info"); walls of text under one heading; broken nesting.
- **Verify.** Generate the document outline; confirm a reader could navigate by headings alone.

<a id="ON-06"></a>
### ON-06 · Primary keyword / intent mapping
- **Definition.** Assigning each URL exactly one primary query intent and maintaining a keyword-to-page map.
- **Why / engines.** Prevents internal competition and concentrates relevance per URL (Google/Bing; clarifies which page AI should cite for a topic).
- **Implement.** Maintain a master map (term → owning URL → intent); one primary intent per page; secondary terms support, never compete.
- **Acceptance.** Every money page has a single assigned primary term · no term assigned to two pages · map is current.
- **Tooling.** A keyword-map spreadsheet, Semrush/Ahrefs (term assignment + difficulty), Search Console (which page actually ranks).
- **Failure modes.** No map; multiple pages targeting the same term (see ON-26); intent mismatch (commercial term on an informational page).
- **Verify.** Search Console: confirm the intended URL is the one ranking for each mapped term, not a sibling.

<a id="ON-07"></a>
### ON-07 · Secondary & semantic term coverage
- **Definition.** Coverage of related entities, synonyms, and the supporting vocabulary a topic implies.
- **Why / engines.** Modern ranking is concept/embedding-based, not keyword-density-based; semantic completeness predicts AI citation (Google/Bing; AI Overviews; LLMs).
- **Implement.** Build the term/entity set from the SERP, "people also ask," and competitor coverage; weave naturally; cover the subtopics a thorough source would.
- **Acceptance.** The page addresses the topic's recognized subtopics/entities · reads naturally · no synonym stuffing.
- **Tooling.** Surfer/Clearscope/MarketMuse (semantic gap), "people also ask" scrape, Semrush topic research.
- **Failure modes.** Treating it as a fill-the-keyword-list exercise; ignoring entities; thin coverage vs. the best competing page.
- **Verify.** Run a content-grader gap analysis vs. top-ranking pages; confirm missing subtopics are added.

<a id="ON-08"></a>
### ON-08 · Search-intent alignment
- **Definition.** Matching the page type and content to the dominant intent behind the query (informational / navigational / commercial / transactional).
- **Why / engines.** Intent mismatch is a top cause of non-ranking regardless of quality (Google/Bing; AI engines select by intent fit).
- **Implement.** Read the current SERP — its format reveals the intent Google rewards (guide vs. product vs. comparison vs. tool); build the page to match that format.
- **Acceptance.** Page format matches the dominant SERP format for the term · satisfies the query without a second click.
- **Tooling.** Manual SERP analysis, Semrush intent labels, "people also ask."
- **Failure modes.** Forcing a sales page onto an informational query; ignoring that intent shifted; mixed intent on one page.
- **Verify.** Compare the page's format against the top 5 ranking results; confirm it's the same archetype.

<a id="ON-09"></a>
### ON-09 · Content depth & comprehensiveness
- **Definition.** Covering the topic as completely as the best available source — breadth and useful detail, not word count.
- **Why / engines.** Comprehensive, self-contained content is rewarded by core updates and is the strongest predictor of AI extraction (Google/Bing; AI Overviews; LLMs).
- **Implement.** Cover every sub-question a searcher would have; answer follow-ups on the page; depth proportional to the topic, not a target length.
- **Acceptance.** No obvious unanswered sub-question · matches or exceeds competing coverage · no padding.
- **Tooling.** Content graders, "people also ask," competitor outline analysis.
- **Failure modes.** Padding to hit a word count (ON-30); breadth without depth; missing the follow-up questions.
- **Verify.** List the searcher's likely follow-ups; confirm the page answers each.

<a id="ON-10"></a>
### ON-10 · Content originality & uniqueness
- **Definition.** Original framing, examples, data, or perspective — not reworded competitor content.
- **Why / engines.** Originality and "information gain" are explicit quality signals; duplicative content is suppressed and rarely cited (Google/Bing; LLMs favor unique sources).
- **Implement.** Add something the SERP lacks — proprietary data, first-hand experience, original examples, a clearer model; never spin existing pages.
- **Acceptance.** Contains ≥1 element not found in competing pages · passes internal/external duplication check · no spun text.
- **Tooling.** Copyscape/Originality.ai (duplication), manual SERP comparison.
- **Failure modes.** Reworded competitor content; templated near-duplicates (ON-29); AI-generated filler with no added value (scaled-content-abuse risk).
- **Verify.** Run a duplication scan; identify the specific original contribution before publishing.

<a id="ON-11"></a>
### ON-11 · Above-the-fold answer placement
- **Definition.** Placing the page's core answer in the opening — within roughly the first 100–150 words / above the fold.
- **Why / engines.** Opening tokens carry disproportionate weight in AI summarization, and front-loaded answers win featured snippets (AI Overviews; LLMs; Google snippets).
- **Implement.** Lead with a direct, self-contained answer (a definition-first or direct-response opening), then expand below; don't bury the answer under preamble.
- **Acceptance.** Core answer present in the first ~100–150 words · the opening reads as a standalone answer.
- **Tooling.** Manual read, snippet-optimization checkers.
- **Failure modes.** Long throat-clearing intros; answer buried mid-page; opening that requires the rest of the page for context.
- **Verify.** Read only the first paragraph in isolation — does it answer the query? If not, rewrite.

<a id="ON-12"></a>
### ON-12 · Natural keyword usage
- **Definition.** Using the target term and variants at a natural frequency, in meaningful positions (title, H1, early body, a heading).
- **Why / engines.** Confirms relevance without triggering spam/over-optimization signals (Google/Bing).
- **Implement.** Place the term where it belongs naturally; vary with synonyms/entities; never hit a density target.
- **Acceptance.** Term appears in key positions · reads naturally aloud · no awkward repetition.
- **Tooling.** Read-aloud test, content grader (as a sanity check, not a quota).
- **Failure modes.** Density targeting; exact-match repetition; stuffing alt text/footers.
- **Verify.** Read the page aloud; flag any repetition that sounds unnatural.

<a id="ON-13"></a>
### ON-13 · Content freshness & update cadence
- **Definition.** Keeping content current and signaling recency via visible dates and substantive updates.
- **Why / engines.** Recency is weighted for time-sensitive topics, and AI engines favor recently-updated sources; refreshing often beats publishing new (Google/Bing; AI Overviews; LLMs — esp. Perplexity).
- **Implement.** Schedule reviews (quarterly for evergreen, faster for volatile topics); update stats/examples/links; show an honest "updated" date only on real changes; retire stale claims.
- **Acceptance.** Money pages reviewed on schedule · "updated" dates reflect real edits · no stale facts.
- **Tooling.** A content calendar, Search Console (decaying pages), analytics (traffic decline).
- **Failure modes.** Fake date-bumps with no content change; letting cornerstone pages decay; updating trivially.
- **Verify.** Audit "updated" dates against changelog/diffs; confirm decaying pages are queued for refresh.

<a id="ON-14"></a>
### ON-14 · Readability & formatting
- **Definition.** Structuring prose for fast comprehension — short paragraphs, plain language, scannable blocks.
- **Why / engines.** Drives engagement/dwell and supports extraction; readers and AI both reward scannable, clear writing (Google/Bing UX signals; LLM comprehension/RAG).
- **Implement.** Short paragraphs (2–4 lines), one idea each; plain words over jargon; bold sparingly for genuine emphasis; use lists/tables where they fit the content.
- **Acceptance.** No wall-of-text blocks · reading level appropriate to audience · structure aids scanning.
- **Tooling.** Hemingway/readability scorers, mobile preview.
- **Failure modes.** Dense academic prose; over-formatting (everything bold); jargon without definition.
- **Verify.** Scan the page on mobile in 10 seconds — can you extract the gist? If not, restructure.

<a id="ON-15"></a>
### ON-15 · Featured-snippet / answer-block formatting
- **Definition.** Formatting discrete answers as snippet-eligible blocks — concise definitions, ordered/unordered lists, comparison tables.
- **Why / engines.** Wins position-zero snippets and is the format AI engines lift verbatim into answers (Google snippets; AI Overviews; LLMs).
- **Implement.** For each answerable sub-question, provide a tight ~40–80 word direct answer, or a clean list/table, under a question-format heading; make each block stand alone.
- **Acceptance.** Key sub-questions have a self-contained answer block (paragraph/list/table) · each block is extraction-ready.
- **Tooling.** "People also ask," snippet trackers (Semrush/Ahrefs SERP features), manual.
- **Failure modes.** Answers embedded in long prose; lists that need surrounding context; tables as images.
- **Verify.** Extract each answer block in isolation — does it fully answer the sub-question with no external context?

<a id="ON-16"></a>
### ON-16 · FAQ sections
- **Definition.** On-page question-and-answer blocks addressing real user questions (paired with FAQPage schema — see TE-31).
- **Why / engines.** Directly answers conversational and long-tail queries; FAQ content is frequently cited and surfaced (AI Overviews; LLMs; Google).
- **Implement.** Use actual questions (from "people also ask," sales calls, search data); concise visible answers (~80–200 words); compose the schema answer independently of the visible text per the L520 split convention.
- **Acceptance.** Questions are real (not invented) · visible answers concise · schema answer composed separately, ≤ ~250 chars.
- **Tooling.** "People also ask," Search Console queries, schema validator.
- **Failure modes.** Padding with fake/obvious questions; answers that don't actually answer; schema/visible mismatch.
- **Verify.** Confirm each question maps to real demand; validate FAQPage markup; check schema-vs-visible independence.

<a id="ON-17"></a>
### ON-17 · Image optimization
- **Definition.** Making images discoverable and performant — descriptive filenames, alt text, captions, compression, correct dimensions.
- **Why / engines.** Image SEO + accessibility + page speed; alt text aids multimodal AI understanding (Google/Bing image search; AI Overviews multimodal; UX).
- **Implement.** Descriptive filenames (panel-upgrade-cypress.jpg, not IMG_001); accurate alt text describing the image (not keyword-stuffed); compress to next-gen formats; serve correct dimensions.
- **Acceptance.** Every meaningful image has descriptive alt · filenames are descriptive · compressed · no oversized files.
- **Tooling.** Screaming Frog (missing alt), Squoosh/ImageOptim, Lighthouse.
- **Failure modes.** Empty/duplicate/stuffed alt; generic filenames; uncompressed hero images (a top LCP killer — see TE-20/25).
- **Verify.** Crawl for missing/duplicate alt; check image weights in Lighthouse.

<a id="ON-18"></a>
### ON-18 · Original / relevant imagery
- **Definition.** Using authentic, relevant images (real work, real team) over generic stock where credibility matters.
- **Why / engines.** Original media supports E-E-A-T/trust and engagement; stock-heavy pages read as low-effort (Google quality signals; UX).
- **Implement.** Use real photography for trust-critical pages (team, work, premises); AI-generated or licensed stock acceptable where appropriate and disclosed per intake `photographyStyle.approach`.
- **Acceptance.** Trust pages use authentic imagery · images are topically relevant · sourcing approach is intentional.
- **Tooling.** Reverse-image check (stock detection), brand asset library.
- **Failure modes.** Obvious stock on About/proof pages; irrelevant decorative images; mismatched style.
- **Verify.** Reverse-image-search hero/trust images; confirm authenticity where it counts.

<a id="ON-19"></a>
### ON-19 · On-page video & media
- **Definition.** Embedded video and rich media, with transcripts and supporting metadata.
- **Why / engines.** Boosts engagement/dwell and multi-format eligibility; transcripts make video content crawlable and AI-extractable (Google/Bing; AI Overviews multimodal; UX).
- **Implement.** Embed relevant video; provide a full transcript on-page; add VideoObject schema; lazy-load to protect LCP.
- **Acceptance.** Video has an on-page transcript · VideoObject schema present · embed doesn't degrade load.
- **Tooling.** YouTube/Vimeo embeds, transcript tools, schema validator, Lighthouse.
- **Failure modes.** Video with no transcript (invisible to crawlers/AI); autoplay; heavy embeds tanking speed.
- **Verify.** Confirm transcript text is in the DOM; validate VideoObject; re-check LCP with the embed present.

<a id="ON-20"></a>
### ON-20 · Contextual internal linking
- **Definition.** In-content links between related pages using descriptive anchors.
- **Why / engines.** Distributes authority, signals topical relationships, and aids discovery/crawl (Google/Bing; helps AI map your topical coverage).
- **Implement.** Link from body copy to related money/cluster pages with natural, descriptive anchors; prioritize links from high-authority pages to priority targets; respect live-only hyperlinking (no links to pre-migration paths in body prose).
- **Acceptance.** Money pages receive contextual internal links · anchors are descriptive · links resolve (live targets only).
- **Tooling.** Screaming Frog (internal link counts, inlinks to target), Search Console (internal links report).
- **Failure modes.** Orphaned money pages (TE-11); generic anchors ("click here"); linking to dev/staging or unmigrated URLs.
- **Verify.** Check inlink count to each priority page; confirm no body links point to non-live paths.

<a id="ON-21"></a>
### ON-21 · Topical internal architecture
- **Definition.** Organizing content as pillar/cluster hubs and spokes that reinforce topical authority.
- **Why / engines.** Demonstrates topical depth and concentrates relevance; well-structured clusters help engines (and AI) recognize you as a topic authority (Google/Bing; LLM topical association).
- **Implement.** Define pillars (broad) and clusters (specific); link spokes up to the pillar and laterally where relevant; one pillar owns each topic.
- **Acceptance.** Each topic has a defined pillar · clusters link to their pillar · no competing pillars for one topic.
- **Tooling.** Site-architecture map, Screaming Frog (link graph), keyword map (ON-06).
- **Failure modes.** Flat structure with no hubs; clusters not linked to pillars; overlapping pillars cannibalizing (ON-26).
- **Verify.** Diagram the cluster; confirm every spoke links to its pillar and the pillar links out to spokes.

<a id="ON-22"></a>
### ON-22 · Outbound links to authoritative sources
- **Definition.** Linking out to credible external sources that support claims.
- **Why / engines.** Supports trust/accuracy and gives readers evidence; reasonable outbound linking is a quality signal (Google/Bing E-E-A-T; supports verifiability AI values).
- **Implement.** Cite authoritative, current sources for stats/claims; link the specific source, not a homepage; open in same tab unless UX dictates otherwise; avoid over-linking.
- **Acceptance.** Non-obvious claims are sourced · links go to authoritative, live pages · linking is judicious.
- **Tooling.** Link checker (dead outbound links), manual source vetting.
- **Failure modes.** No sourcing on factual claims; linking low-quality/competitor pages; excessive outbound links; dead links.
- **Verify.** Confirm each stat/claim has a credible source link; run a broken-outbound-link check.

<a id="ON-23"></a>
### ON-23 · Internal anchor-text optimization
- **Definition.** The clickable text of internal links — descriptive and varied, non-manipulative.
- **Why / engines.** Anchor text signals the target page's topic to engines (Google/Bing).
- **Implement.** Describe the destination ("panel upgrade service," not "here"); vary phrasing naturally; never force exact-match on every internal link.
- **Acceptance.** Internal anchors are descriptive · varied · no robotic exact-match repetition.
- **Tooling.** Screaming Frog (anchor text report).
- **Failure modes.** "Click here"/"read more"; identical exact-match anchor on every link to a page; keyword-stuffed anchors.
- **Verify.** Export internal anchors per target page; check for descriptiveness and natural variation.

<a id="ON-24"></a>
### ON-24 · Table of contents / jump links
- **Definition.** An on-page TOC with anchor links to sections, on long content.
- **Why / engines.** Improves navigation/UX and can earn jump-to-section snippet links in SERPs (Google; UX).
- **Implement.** Add a TOC for long-form pages; use anchor links to properly-IDed headings; keep it sticky on desktop where helpful.
- **Acceptance.** Long pages have a working TOC · anchors jump to the correct sections · accessible.
- **Tooling.** CMS TOC block/plugin, manual anchor check.
- **Failure modes.** Broken/missing anchor IDs; TOC on short pages that don't need it; inaccessible jump links.
- **Verify.** Click each TOC link; confirm it jumps to the right heading.

<a id="ON-25"></a>
### ON-25 · Main-content prominence
- **Definition.** Ensuring primary content dominates the viewport and the DOM over chrome, ads, and interstitials.
- **Why / engines.** Page-experience and "main content" quality signals reward content-forward layouts; intrusive elements are penalized (Google page experience; UX).
- **Implement.** Keep the core content above and central; limit ads/popups; ensure the primary content is the bulk of the rendered page, not boilerplate.
- **Acceptance.** Main content visible without excessive scrolling past chrome/ads · no content-blocking interstitials (see UX-08).
- **Tooling.** Manual viewport review (mobile + desktop), Lighthouse.
- **Failure modes.** Content pushed below ads/sliders; high boilerplate-to-content ratio; intrusive popups.
- **Verify.** Load the page fresh on mobile; confirm primary content is reachable without dismissing overlays or heavy scrolling.

<a id="ON-26"></a>
### ON-26 · Keyword cannibalization control
- **Definition.** Preventing multiple pages from targeting the same primary intent.
- **Why / engines.** Cannibalization splits signals and confuses which page should rank/be cited (Google/Bing; AI source selection).
- **Implement.** Audit the keyword map (ON-06) for collisions; consolidate/merge or 301 the weaker page, or re-differentiate intents; pick one canonical owner per term.
- **Acceptance.** No primary term owned by two URLs · collisions resolved by merge/redirect/differentiation.
- **Tooling.** Search Console (multiple URLs ranking for one query), Ahrefs/Semrush, keyword map.
- **Failure modes.** Multiple near-duplicate service pages; old blog posts competing with money pages; ignoring the collision.
- **Verify.** Search Console: for each key term, confirm a single intended URL is ranking, not several rotating ones.

<a id="ON-27"></a>
### ON-27 · Semantic HTML for content
- **Definition.** Using correct HTML elements for meaning — lists, tables, definition lists, emphasis, figures.
- **Why / engines.** Helps parsers and assistive tech interpret structure; clean semantics aid AI extraction (Google/Bing; LLM/RAG; accessibility).
- **Implement.** Real `<ul>/<ol>` for lists, `<table>` for tabular data, `<dl>` for definitions, `<figure>/<figcaption>` for media, headings for structure — not styled `<div>`s faking it.
- **Acceptance.** Lists/tables/definitions use proper elements · no faux-structure via styled divs · valid markup.
- **Tooling.** Browser inspector, W3C validator, axe accessibility.
- **Failure modes.** Tabular data in divs; "lists" as line-broken paragraphs; tables used for layout.
- **Verify.** Inspect a list/table in DevTools; confirm it uses the correct element.

<a id="ON-28"></a>
### ON-28 · Multimedia diversity
- **Definition.** Combining text with images, video, and data visualizations on key pages.
- **Why / engines.** Mixed-format pages have higher selection odds in AI answers and better engagement (AI Overviews multimodal; Google; UX).
- **Implement.** Add the format that genuinely aids understanding — a diagram for a process, a chart for data, a clip for a demo; don't add media for its own sake.
- **Acceptance.** Key pages use ≥2 content formats where it aids comprehension · media is substantive, not decorative.
- **Tooling.** Charting tools, diagramming tools, the image/video items (ON-17/19).
- **Failure modes.** Text-only cornerstone pages; decorative media that adds nothing; media that hurts speed (TE-25).
- **Verify.** Confirm priority pages pair prose with a relevant visual or data element.

<a id="ON-29"></a>
### ON-29 · Templated / location-page uniqueness
- **Definition.** Differentiating programmatically-generated pages (locations, services) so they aren't near-duplicates.
- **Why / engines.** Thin, templated duplicates risk doorway-page and scaled-content-abuse treatment (Google spam policies; weak AI/citation value). Directly relevant to multi-location partner work.
- **Implement.** Give each location/service page genuinely unique content — local specifics, distinct proof, area-relevant detail — not a find-and-replace of the city name.
- **Acceptance.** Each templated page has substantive unique content · passes duplication check · no city-swap doorways.
- **Tooling.** Duplication scanner, Screaming Frog (near-dupe detection), manual sampling.
- **Failure modes.** City-name-swap pages; identical service descriptions across locations; mass-generated thin pages.
- **Verify.** Diff two location pages; confirm meaningful unique content beyond the place name.

<a id="ON-30"></a>
### ON-30 · Word count appropriate to intent
- **Definition.** Length driven by what fully satisfies the intent — sufficiency, not a target number.
- **Why / engines.** There is no ranking bonus for length; padding dilutes and can trigger unhelpful-content signals (Google/Bing).
- **Implement.** Write until the intent is fully served, then stop; match the depth the SERP rewards; cut filler.
- **Acceptance.** Length matches intent · no padding · nothing essential omitted.
- **Tooling.** Competitor length as a reference (not a target), content grader.
- **Failure modes.** Hitting an arbitrary word count; padding transactional pages; truncating topics that need depth.
- **Verify.** Could you cut 20% without losing value? If yes, it's padded. Could the reader still have questions? If yes, it's thin.

<a id="ON-31"></a>
### ON-31 · In-content data, statistics & citations
- **Definition.** Specific, sourced data points and statistics within the content.
- **Why / engines.** Fact-dense, specific, sourced content is disproportionately cited by AI and supports E-E-A-T (LLMs; AI Overviews; Google trust). Hard constraint: no fabricated stats.
- **Implement.** Use concrete numbers/dates with credible sources (ON-22); prefer proprietary/original data where you have it (highly citable); attribute clearly.
- **Acceptance.** Claims are specific and sourced · zero fabricated or unverifiable stats · original data attributed.
- **Tooling.** Source vetting, the correction log (Before/After/Source per claim), fact-check pass.
- **Failure modes.** Vague assertions ("studies show"); invented percentages; unsourced figures; stale stats.
- **Verify.** Every stat traces to a named, current source in the correction log before publishing.

<a id="ON-32"></a>
### ON-32 · Author attribution / byline
- **Definition.** A named, credentialed author on substantive content.
- **Why / engines.** Authorship is a direct E-E-A-T signal and an entity AI engines weight when assessing trust (Google E-E-A-T; LLMs).
- **Implement.** Apply the Allegiant byline standard — Chad Markham + Mike Small (Dir. Production) for SEO/AI SEO pages (add the current SEO lead once designated); Chad Markham + Mike Small + Will Holt (SEM Manager) for SEM pages — each with a real author bio and Person schema (EE-05/06).
- **Acceptance.** Substantive pages carry the correct byline per content type · author has a real bio + Person schema · no anonymous cornerstone content.
- **Tooling.** CMS author fields, Person schema validator.
- **Failure modes.** Anonymous or "admin" bylines; wrong byline for content type; byline with no backing bio/entity.
- **Verify.** Confirm the byline matches the content-type rule and links to a real author page with Person schema.

<a id="ON-33"></a>
### ON-33 · Visible publish & last-updated timestamps
- **Definition.** Showing accurate publish and last-updated dates on content.
- **Why / engines.** Transparency + freshness signal; AI engines read byline/URL/timestamp dates to assess currency (Google; AI Overviews; LLMs).
- **Implement.** Display publish date and an honest "updated" date; keep `datePublished`/`dateModified` in schema consistent with the visible dates; update only on real changes (ties to ON-13).
- **Acceptance.** Visible dates present and accurate · schema dates match visible dates · no fake bumps.
- **Tooling.** CMS date fields, schema validator, changelog.
- **Failure modes.** Hidden dates; schema/visible date mismatch; cosmetic date-bumping; missing dateModified.
- **Verify.** Compare visible dates to schema and to the actual edit history.

<a id="ON-34"></a>
### ON-34 · Breadcrumb navigation
- **Definition.** A breadcrumb trail showing the page's place in the hierarchy (with BreadcrumbList schema).
- **Why / engines.** Aids orientation/UX and produces breadcrumb rich results in SERPs (Google; UX).
- **Implement.** Add breadcrumbs reflecting true site hierarchy; mark up with BreadcrumbList schema; keep labels concise and accurate.
- **Acceptance.** Breadcrumbs present on deep pages · reflect real hierarchy · BreadcrumbList schema validates.
- **Tooling.** CMS breadcrumb module, Rich Results Test.
- **Failure modes.** Breadcrumbs that don't match structure; missing schema; breadcrumbs on a flat site that add nothing.
- **Verify.** Validate BreadcrumbList in Rich Results Test; confirm the trail matches the actual path.

<a id="ON-35"></a>
### ON-35 · Pagination & series handling
- **Definition.** Correct handling of paginated sequences and multi-part content for crawl and UX.
- **Why / engines.** Ensures all items in a series/archive are discoverable and not duplicated or trapped (Google/Bing crawl; UX). Coordinates with TE-41.
- **Implement.** Provide crawlable pagination links (real `<a href>`); give each page a self-referencing canonical; ensure paginated/infinite-scroll content has crawlable URLs; keep titles distinct per page.
- **Acceptance.** All series items reachable via crawlable links · each page self-canonical · no infinite-scroll content hidden from crawlers.
- **Tooling.** Screaming Frog (pagination crawl), Search Console (coverage of paginated URLs).
- **Failure modes.** JS-only "load more" with no URLs; canonicalizing all pages to page 1 (drops deep items); duplicate titles across pages.
- **Verify.** Crawl the paginated set; confirm deep items are indexed and reachable without JS.

## Part 2 · Off-Page / Off-Site (OF-01 – OF-32)

**Template per item:** Definition · Why it matters (+ engines) · Implement · Acceptance · Tooling · Failure modes · Verify.

<a id="OF-01"></a>
### OF-01 · Referring-domain count & diversity
- **Definition.** The count of *unique domains* linking to the site — more predictive than raw link total.
- **Why / engines.** Among the strongest correlates of organic ranking; breadth signals broad endorsement (Google/Bing). Feeds OMNIVIZ MCN.
- **Implement.** Pursue links from many distinct, relevant domains over many links from few; track referring-domain growth, not link counts.
- **Acceptance.** Referring-domain count trending up · diversity across distinct domains · not over-reliant on a handful of sites.
- **Tooling.** Ahrefs/Semrush (referring domains over time), Search Console (links report).
- **Failure modes.** Chasing link count over domain count; hundreds of links from one network; site-wide footer links inflating totals.
- **Verify.** Check the referring-domain *trend* (not total links) over the last 6–12 months.

<a id="OF-02"></a>
### OF-02 · Linking-domain authority
- **Definition.** The trust/strength of the domains linking to you (DR/DA-style metrics as proxies).
- **Why / engines.** A link from a high-authority, trusted domain passes more value than many weak ones (Google/Bing).
- **Implement.** Prioritize outreach to authoritative, established, topically-relevant sites; weight quality over volume.
- **Acceptance.** New links skew toward credible/authoritative domains · profile not dominated by low-quality sources.
- **Tooling.** Ahrefs (DR), Moz (DA), Semrush (Authority Score) — directional proxies, not Google's own metrics.
- **Failure modes.** Treating all links as equal; high-DR but irrelevant sites; forgetting DR/DA are third-party estimates.
- **Verify.** Sample new referring domains for authority *and* relevance, not volume alone.

<a id="OF-03"></a>
### OF-03 · Backlink topical relevance
- **Definition.** Links from pages/sites topically related to yours.
- **Why / engines.** Relevance amplifies a link's value; context matters as much as authority (Google/Bing). Reinforces the topical authority AI weighs.
- **Implement.** Target sites/pages in your niche or adjacent; a relevant mid-authority link often beats an irrelevant high-authority one.
- **Acceptance.** Majority of new links from topically relevant contexts · context aligns with the linked page's subject.
- **Tooling.** Ahrefs/Semrush (referring page topic), manual review.
- **Failure modes.** Random high-DR links with no fit; generic directories; off-topic guest posts.
- **Verify.** Review referring pages for topical alignment with the target page.

<a id="OF-04"></a>
### OF-04 · Anchor-text profile health
- **Definition.** The distribution of inbound anchor text — branded, naked URL, exact-match, partial, generic.
- **Why / engines.** A natural distribution avoids over-optimization; engineered exact-match at scale is a link-spam signal (Google/Bing).
- **Implement.** Let anchors skew natural — mostly branded/naked/generic, sparing exact-match; you can't fully control inbound anchors, so don't engineer exact-match.
- **Acceptance.** Branded + naked dominate · exact-match a small minority · no unnatural exact-match spike.
- **Tooling.** Ahrefs/Semrush (anchor distribution).
- **Failure modes.** Over-optimized commercial anchors (classic penalty trigger); paid-link anchors; sudden anchor spikes.
- **Verify.** Pull the anchor distribution; confirm exact-match is a small, naturally-trending share.

<a id="OF-05"></a>
### OF-05 · Dofollow / nofollow balance
- **Definition.** The mix of followed vs. nofollow/ugc/sponsored attributes among inbound links.
- **Why / engines.** A 100%-dofollow profile looks unnatural; real sites produce a mix, which signals organic acquisition (Google/Bing).
- **Implement.** Don't obsess over attributes — nofollow links still drive traffic, exposure, and discovery; a natural profile contains both.
- **Acceptance.** Profile shows a natural mix · not engineered all-dofollow · nofollow valued for traffic/exposure.
- **Tooling.** Ahrefs/Semrush (follow vs. nofollow split).
- **Failure modes.** Discarding nofollow opportunities; treating nofollow as worthless; an unnaturally all-followed profile.
- **Verify.** Compare your follow/nofollow ratio against competitor profiles for naturalness.

<a id="OF-06"></a>
### OF-06 · Link velocity
- **Definition.** The rate of new-link acquisition over time.
- **Why / engines.** A steady, sustained pace is healthy; inexplicable spikes can draw scrutiny (Google/Bing).
- **Implement.** Build steadily; expect spikes around genuine PR/news (those read as natural); avoid bursts from paid/spam sources.
- **Acceptance.** Pace steady or tied to real events · no unexplained spikes from low-quality sources.
- **Tooling.** Ahrefs/Semrush (new/lost links over time).
- **Failure modes.** Burst-buying links; mistaking a paid spike for momentum; ignoring sudden link loss.
- **Verify.** Review the new-links timeline; confirm spikes map to real campaigns/news.

<a id="OF-07"></a>
### OF-07 · Editorial / earned links
- **Definition.** Links given voluntarily because content merits citation — vs. paid, placed, or self-made.
- **Why / engines.** The gold standard Google's guidelines endorse; self-made/paid links risk devaluation or penalty (Google/Bing).
- **Implement.** Earn links by being citation-worthy (original data, expertise, useful tools); outreach pitches genuine value, not payment or swaps.
- **Acceptance.** New links predominantly editorial · no paid-for-rankings links · acquisition is value-driven.
- **Tooling.** Manual link-context review, Ahrefs (referring page context).
- **Failure modes.** Buying links; link exchanges at scale; passing off sponsored content as editorial.
- **Verify.** Sample recent links; confirm editorial placement, not payment or swap.

<a id="OF-08"></a>
### OF-08 · Guest & contributed articles
- **Definition.** Authoritative, relevant contributed content on third-party publications.
- **Why / engines.** Done well: authority, referral traffic, author entity. Done at scale: a flagged link scheme (Google/Bing). Supports EAB.
- **Implement.** Contribute genuinely useful articles to relevant, quality publications; natural in-content/author-bio links; tie to the author entity (EE-06); quality over quantity.
- **Acceptance.** Placements on relevant, quality sites · substantive content · natural links · not mass-produced.
- **Tooling.** Outreach/CRM tools, publication vetting (traffic/authority/relevance).
- **Failure modes.** Mass low-quality guest posting; irrelevant sites; thin link-only articles; over-optimized anchors.
- **Verify.** Confirm each placement is a vetted, relevant publication with real editorial standards.

<a id="OF-09"></a>
### OF-09 · Digital PR & media coverage
- **Definition.** Earning press coverage and links through newsworthy stories, data, and campaigns.
- **Why / engines.** The dominant white-hat strategy for high-authority links *and* brand mentions; coverage feeds the entity/brand signals AI weighs (Google/Bing; LLMs; AI Overviews).
- **Implement.** Build newsworthy hooks (original research, expert commentary, timely angles); pitch journalists; target authoritative outlets; map outcomes to MCN.
- **Acceptance.** Campaigns earn coverage on credible outlets · coverage carries brand mention (ideally a link) · stories genuinely newsworthy.
- **Tooling.** Media databases (Muck Rack/Cision-class), sourcing services (OF-29), Ahrefs (coverage links).
- **Failure modes.** Non-newsworthy "PR" that earns nothing; spray-and-pray pitching; PR with no real story.
- **Verify.** Track coverage per campaign; confirm placements are authoritative outlets.

<a id="OF-10"></a>
### OF-10 · Linkable assets
- **Definition.** Content built to attract links — original research, data studies, free tools, calculators, definitive guides.
- **Why / engines.** The supply side of link earning; gives others a reason to cite you, and original data is highly AI-citable (Google/Bing; LLMs).
- **Implement.** Produce proprietary data, tools, or definitive references in your niche; promote via PR/outreach; design them to *be* the citable source.
- **Acceptance.** ≥1 genuine linkable asset per priority topic · original/useful · actively promoted.
- **Tooling.** Survey/data tools, dev resources for tools/calculators, design.
- **Failure modes.** Thin/derivative "assets"; building without promoting; no original angle.
- **Verify.** Confirm each priority topic has a citable asset that's earning links/mentions.

<a id="OF-11"></a>
### OF-11 · Resource-page link building
- **Definition.** Earning placement on curated "resources/links" pages in your niche.
- **Why / engines.** Relevant, often authoritative contextual links (Google/Bing).
- **Implement.** Find relevant resource pages via search operators; ensure you have a genuinely worthy resource; pitch its inclusion.
- **Acceptance.** Placements on relevant, maintained resource pages · your linked asset genuinely fits.
- **Tooling.** Search operators (inurl:resources / inurl:links), Ahrefs (competitors' resource-page links), outreach.
- **Failure modes.** Pitching weak resources; dead/spammy resource pages; irrelevant lists.
- **Verify.** Confirm the resource page is live, relevant, and maintained.

<a id="OF-12"></a>
### OF-12 · Broken-link building
- **Definition.** Finding dead links on relevant pages and offering your equivalent as the replacement.
- **Why / engines.** Mutually useful, white-hat tactic for relevant links (Google/Bing).
- **Implement.** Identify broken outbound links on relevant pages; ensure you have a matching/better resource; notify the owner with the fix.
- **Acceptance.** Replacements genuinely equivalent/better · target pages relevant · outreach helpful, not spammy.
- **Tooling.** Ahrefs / Check My Links (broken-link finders), outreach.
- **Failure modes.** Pitching a poor substitute; irrelevant pages; mass low-value outreach.
- **Verify.** Confirm the broken link existed and your resource is a legitimate replacement.

<a id="OF-13"></a>
### OF-13 · Link & mention reclamation
- **Definition.** Recovering lost links and converting unlinked brand mentions into links.
- **Why / engines.** Low-effort, high-relevance gains; unlinked mentions already show editorial intent (Google/Bing; converting them tightens entity signals).
- **Implement.** Monitor for lost links (reach out to restore) and unlinked brand/product mentions (request a link); reclaim uncredited asset/image uses.
- **Acceptance.** Lost links reviewed/recovered where feasible · unlinked mentions pursued · monitoring ongoing.
- **Tooling.** Ahrefs (lost links), brand-mention monitoring (Mention/Brand24/Google Alerts).
- **Failure modes.** Not monitoring mentions; ignoring lost links; aggressive demands that backfire.
- **Verify.** Run lost-links + unlinked-mentions scans; confirm a follow-up cadence exists.

<a id="OF-14"></a>
### OF-14 · Competitor backlink-gap analysis
- **Definition.** Identifying domains that link to competitors but not to you, then pursuing the replicable ones.
- **Why / engines.** Surfaces proven, relevant link opportunities (Google/Bing).
- **Implement.** Run a link intersect across top competitors; filter for relevant, attainable, quality domains; prioritize domains linking to multiple competitors.
- **Acceptance.** Gap analysis run regularly · attainable targets identified and worked · prioritized by relevance/authority.
- **Tooling.** Ahrefs Link Intersect, Semrush Backlink Gap.
- **Failure modes.** Chasing unattainable links; replicating competitors' spam; ignoring relevance.
- **Verify.** Produce a current gap list; confirm an active pipeline against the attainable subset.

<a id="OF-15"></a>
### OF-15 · On-page link placement
- **Definition.** Where the link sits on the linking page — in-content body vs. footer/sidebar/author-box.
- **Why / engines.** In-content, contextual links carry more weight than boilerplate placements (Google/Bing).
- **Implement.** Prioritize earning in-content editorial links; value contextual placement over site-wide boilerplate.
- **Acceptance.** Priority links are in-content/contextual · not reliant on footer/sidebar site-wides.
- **Tooling.** Manual review, Ahrefs (link location).
- **Failure modes.** Site-wide footer link networks; sidebar link farms; counting placements regardless of position.
- **Verify.** Inspect where key inbound links appear on their pages.

<a id="OF-16"></a>
### OF-16 · Linking-page authority
- **Definition.** The authority/relevance of the specific linking *page*, not just its domain.
- **Why / engines.** Page-level signals matter; a strong relevant page beats a weak page on a strong domain (Google/Bing).
- **Implement.** Target links on pages with their own authority/traffic/relevance, not any page on a high-DR domain.
- **Acceptance.** Key links sit on pages with genuine authority/relevance, not orphaned/thin pages.
- **Tooling.** Ahrefs (URL Rating / page traffic), manual.
- **Failure modes.** Links on orphaned, no-traffic, thin pages; domain-only thinking.
- **Verify.** Check the linking page's own authority/traffic, not just the domain's.

<a id="OF-17"></a>
### OF-17 · Toxic-link identification & disavow
- **Definition.** Identifying manipulative/spammy inbound links and, where warranted, disavowing them.
- **Why / engines.** Google largely ignores spam links algorithmically, but disavow still matters for known paid/manipulative profiles or negative SEO; misuse can harm you (Google/Bing).
- **Implement.** Audit the profile periodically; disavow only clearly manipulative links you can't get removed (especially self-/predecessor-built); use surgically — Google says most sites never need it.
- **Acceptance.** Profile audited · disavow reserved for genuinely toxic/self-made spam · no over-disavowing healthy links.
- **Tooling.** Search Console (disavow tool + links report), Ahrefs/Semrush toxicity flags (directional, not gospel).
- **Failure modes.** Disavowing healthy links out of fear; ignoring a genuinely manipulative legacy profile; trusting toxicity scores blindly.
- **Verify.** Before disavowing, confirm links are genuinely manipulative and removal attempts failed.

<a id="OF-18"></a>
### OF-18 · Brand mentions (linked & unlinked)
- **Definition.** Mentions of the brand across the web, with or without a link.
- **Why / engines.** Unlinked mentions feed entity/brand signals; mentions across trusted sites are a strong AI-citation driver and increasingly weighted by Google (Google brand/entity; LLMs; AI Overviews). Core to MCN/EAB.
- **Implement.** Earn mentions broadly (PR, partnerships, community, reviews); keep brand facts consistent everywhere (EE-26); monitor and convert to links where useful (OF-13).
- **Acceptance.** Mention footprint growing across trusted sites · facts consistent · mentions monitored.
- **Tooling.** Mention/Brand24/Google Alerts, Ahrefs (mentions), AI-citation trackers (Part 9).
- **Failure modes.** Valuing only links; inconsistent brand facts; no mention monitoring.
- **Verify.** Run a brand-mention scan; confirm footprint growth and cross-source consistency.

<a id="OF-19"></a>
### OF-19 · Branded search volume
- **Definition.** How many people search for your brand by name.
- **Why / engines.** A powerful authority/demand signal — Google associates branded search with legitimacy, and AI familiarity correlates with brand prevalence (Google; LLMs). A top-tier AI-visibility driver.
- **Implement.** Grow branded demand via PR, content, social, advertising, and offline presence; branded search is the downstream measure that brand-building is working.
- **Acceptance.** Branded search trending up · brand SERP owned (rank #1 for your name, control the knowledge panel).
- **Tooling.** Search Console (branded queries), Google Trends, Semrush (branded volume).
- **Failure modes.** Dismissing brand-building as "not SEO"; not owning the branded SERP; confusion with similarly-named entities.
- **Verify.** Track branded-query trend; confirm you own page one for your brand name.

<a id="OF-20"></a>
### OF-20 · Co-citation & co-occurrence
- **Definition.** Being mentioned alongside relevant topics/competitors, even without a direct link.
- **Why / engines.** Associates your brand with a topic/category in engines' and models' understanding (Google semantic association; LLM entity association).
- **Implement.** Get into "best X" lists, comparisons, and topic roundups alongside category leaders; place the brand in the right topical company.
- **Acceptance.** Brand appears in relevant category/comparison contexts · associated with target topics across sources.
- **Tooling.** Manual SERP/list research, AI-citation trackers, mention monitoring.
- **Failure modes.** Absent from lists where competitors appear; associated with the wrong topics; no comparison-content presence.
- **Verify.** Search category and "best of" queries; confirm the brand appears alongside peers.

<a id="OF-21"></a>
### OF-21 · Social profile presence & activity
- **Definition.** Claimed, complete, active profiles on relevant social platforms.
- **Why / engines.** Social links are largely nofollow and not a direct ranking factor, but profiles aid brand/entity signals, discovery, branded search, and brand-SERP control; some platforms feed AI (Google brand signals; LLMs via some platforms).
- **Implement.** Claim and complete relevant profiles (consistent NAP/branding); maintain genuine activity on the channels your audience uses; don't spread thin across irrelevant ones.
- **Acceptance.** Relevant profiles claimed + complete + consistent · active where it matters · no dormant key profiles.
- **Tooling.** Profile audit, social management tools.
- **Failure modes.** Dormant profiles (the Tucker/Armstrong A.R.C. pattern); inconsistent NAP; chasing every platform.
- **Verify.** Audit each profile for completeness, consistency, and recent activity.

<a id="OF-22"></a>
### OF-22 · Influencer & partnership mentions
- **Definition.** Credible third parties (influencers, partners, vendors) mentioning/endorsing the brand.
- **Why / engines.** Third-party endorsement builds trust, mentions, and sometimes links; strengthens entity associations (Google brand signals; LLMs).
- **Implement.** Build genuine relationships with relevant influencers/partners; co-marketing, partnerships, customer/vendor features; disclose paid relationships per FTC.
- **Acceptance.** Credible third parties endorse the brand · relationships genuine · disclosures compliant.
- **Tooling.** Mention monitoring, outreach/CRM, partnership tracking.
- **Failure modes.** Irrelevant/inauthentic endorsements; undisclosed paid promotion (FTC risk); bought engagement.
- **Verify.** Confirm endorsements are genuine and properly disclosed.

<a id="OF-23"></a>
### OF-23 · Podcast & interview appearances
- **Definition.** Appearing as a guest expert on podcasts, webinars, and interviews.
- **Why / engines.** Builds author/brand authority, mentions, show-notes links, and entity signals; transcripts add discoverable content (Google E-E-A-T/brand; LLMs).
- **Implement.** Pitch relevant shows; bring genuine expertise; ensure show notes link/mention you; repurpose transcripts on your own site.
- **Acceptance.** Regular relevant appearances · show-notes mentions/links earned · expertise tied to the author entity.
- **Tooling.** Podcast-guesting platforms, outreach, transcript repurposing.
- **Failure modes.** Irrelevant shows; no link/mention follow-through; not repurposing the content.
- **Verify.** Confirm appearances earn mentions/links and reinforce the author entity.

<a id="OF-24"></a>
### OF-24 · Forum & community participation
- **Definition.** Genuine, value-first participation in Reddit, Quora, and niche communities.
- **Why / engines.** Community content — Reddit especially — is heavily cited by AI engines and surfaces in Google; builds brand/entity presence (LLMs, esp. Perplexity; Google; brand signals).
- **Implement.** Participate authentically where your audience is; answer with real value; mention/link only where relevant and allowed; never spam or astroturf.
- **Acceptance.** Genuine helpful presence in relevant communities · contributions add value · no spam/astroturfing.
- **Tooling.** Reddit/Quora, community + brand-mention monitoring.
- **Failure modes.** Self-promotional spam (banned, backfires); astroturfing (integrity + platform risk); irrelevant communities.
- **Verify.** Confirm participation reads as genuinely helpful and complies with community rules.

<a id="OF-25"></a>
### OF-25 · Q&A / answer-platform presence
- **Definition.** Authoritative answers on Q&A platforms (Quora, niche/industry Q&A).
- **Why / engines.** Builds topical authority and entity presence; answers can rank and be cited (Google; LLMs).
- **Implement.** Answer relevant questions with genuine depth; position the author as an expert; link only where it adds value.
- **Acceptance.** Substantive answers on relevant questions · author positioned as expert · value-first.
- **Tooling.** Quora and niche Q&A platforms, monitoring.
- **Failure modes.** Thin promotional answers; link-dropping; irrelevant questions.
- **Verify.** Confirm answers are substantive and build genuine authority.

<a id="OF-26"></a>
### OF-26 · Industry directory & association listings
- **Definition.** Listings in relevant, authoritative directories and trade/industry associations.
- **Why / engines.** Relevant citations and trust signals; association membership signals legitimacy and feeds entity/local signals (Google local + brand; AI). The legitimate cousin of mass-directory spam.
- **Implement.** List in vetted, relevant directories; join genuine industry associations (and their member directories); keep NAP consistent.
- **Acceptance.** Listed in relevant authoritative directories/associations · NAP consistent · no spammy mass submissions.
- **Tooling.** Directory/citation tools (BrightLocal-class), association memberships.
- **Failure modes.** Mass low-quality directory submissions; inconsistent NAP; paying for irrelevant listings.
- **Verify.** Confirm listings are relevant and authoritative with consistent NAP.

<a id="OF-27"></a>
### OF-27 · Wikipedia / Wikidata entity presence
- **Definition.** A Wikipedia article and/or Wikidata entry establishing the brand/person as a recognized entity (notability permitting).
- **Why / engines.** Foundational entity references feeding Google's Knowledge Graph and heavily used in AI grounding/training; among the most-cited sources (Google Knowledge Graph; LLMs — ChatGPT favors Wikipedia-class sources).
- **Implement.** Pursue *only* if genuinely notable (independent coverage exists); follow Wikipedia policy strictly (no paid-editing violations); keep Wikidata accurate; build the independent coverage that supports notability first.
- **Acceptance.** Entity present where notability supports it · entries accurate and policy-compliant · Wikidata facts correct.
- **Tooling.** Wikidata, Wikipedia (strict policy adherence), Knowledge Graph check.
- **Failure modes.** Creating non-notable articles (deleted, reputational risk); paid-editing violations; inaccurate Wikidata.
- **Verify.** Confirm notability is genuine and entries comply with policy before pursuing.

<a id="OF-28"></a>
### OF-28 · Off-site reviews & ratings footprint
- **Definition.** Volume, quality, and recency of reviews across platforms (Google, industry-specific, BBB, etc.).
- **Why / engines.** A trust/reputation signal weighted by Google (especially local) and AI; review content is mined for sentiment and citation (Google local + brand; LLMs). Ties to LO-08/09.
- **Implement.** Earn steady genuine reviews on the platforms that matter for the vertical; respond professionally; never fake or incentivize against platform rules; maintain rating and recency.
- **Acceptance.** Steady genuine review flow · healthy rating · responses present · no fake/policy-violating reviews.
- **Tooling.** Review-management tools, GBP, industry review platforms.
- **Failure modes.** Fake/incentivized reviews (legal + platform risk); ignoring negatives; stale reviews; rating below competitors (the Tucker/Armstrong pattern).
- **Verify.** Audit review volume/rating/recency vs. competitors; confirm acquisition is genuine.

<a id="OF-29"></a>
### OF-29 · Expert sourcing (HARO-style)
- **Definition.** Providing expert quotes to journalists via sourcing services, earning mentions/links in published pieces.
- **Why / engines.** Earns authoritative coverage, links, and author-entity authority (Google E-E-A-T/PR; LLMs).
- **Implement.** Monitor relevant queries (Connectively/Qwoted/Featured-class services); respond fast with genuine expertise; tie quotes to the author entity.
- **Acceptance.** Regular expert placements · credible outlets · reinforcing the author entity.
- **Tooling.** Connectively / Qwoted / Featured-style platforms.
- **Failure modes.** Generic/slow responses that don't land; irrelevant queries; quotes with no real expertise.
- **Verify.** Track placements; confirm outlets are credible and quotes attributed to the expert.

<a id="OF-30"></a>
### OF-30 · High-trust domain links
- **Definition.** Links from .edu, .gov, and recognized institutional/high-authority domains, where legitimate.
- **Why / engines.** Strong trust signals when genuinely earned (Google/Bing).
- **Implement.** Earn legitimately — scholarships, genuine resources, partnerships, original research cited by institutions; never buy "edu links."
- **Acceptance.** Any such links legitimately earned and relevant · no purchased/scheme links.
- **Tooling.** Ahrefs (filter by TLD), outreach.
- **Failure modes.** Buying .edu links/schemes (devalued, risky); irrelevant institutional links; over-indexing on TLD over relevance.
- **Verify.** Confirm institutional links are genuinely earned and contextually relevant.

<a id="OF-31"></a>
### OF-31 · Sponsorships & events generating mentions
- **Definition.** Sponsoring or participating in events/organizations that generate brand mentions and links.
- **Why / engines.** Earns local/industry mentions, links, and exposure; supports local prominence and entity signals (Google local + brand; LLMs).
- **Implement.** Sponsor relevant local/industry events, charities, teams; ensure listings link/mention you; align to the markets/verticals you serve.
- **Acceptance.** Sponsorships earn genuine mentions/links · relevant to market/vertical · not pay-for-link schemes.
- **Tooling.** Sponsorship tracking, mention monitoring.
- **Failure modes.** Sponsorships purely for links (treated as paid links if commercially anchored); irrelevant events; no follow-through.
- **Verify.** Confirm sponsorships yield genuine mentions/links and fit the market.

<a id="OF-32"></a>
### OF-32 · Press-release distribution
- **Definition.** Distributing press releases for genuine news, used judiciously.
- **Why / engines.** PR-wire links are typically nofollow and Google treats syndicated PR links as self-made (little ranking value) — but releases can earn genuine pickup, mentions, and brand visibility (Google: links discounted; brand/mention value real).
- **Implement.** Use only for genuine news; expect brand/mention/visibility value, not link equity; never use PR as a link tactic or stuff commercial anchors. Prefer earned coverage (OF-09) over wire distribution.
- **Acceptance.** Releases reserved for real news · link-value expectations set low · no anchor stuffing/link-scheme use.
- **Tooling.** Wire services (genuine news only), media outreach (preferred — OF-09).
- **Failure modes.** Treating PR wires as a link channel; over-distributing non-news; commercial anchor stuffing (scheme signal).
- **Verify.** Confirm releases are genuine news and aren't functioning as a link scheme.

## Part 3 · Technical SEO (TE-01 – TE-42)

**Template per item:** Definition · Why it matters (+ engines) · Implement · Acceptance · Tooling · Failure modes · Verify.
**Conventions:** Binary acceptance checks where possible. No fabricated stats. "Engines" = which surfaces the factor moves (Google/Bing organic, AI Overviews/AI Mode, LLM citation, rich results).

<a id="TE-01"></a>
### TE-01 · robots.txt configuration
- **Definition.** The root file directing crawler access to paths and pointing to the sitemap.
- **Why / engines.** Controls crawl access; a missing or misconfigured file blocks indexing or wastes crawl budget (Google/Bing; AI crawlers read it too — ties to AI-13/14).
- **Implement.** Valid file at root returning 200; allow important paths; disallow only what should never be crawled (never block CSS/JS needed to render); reference the XML sitemap; set AI-crawler directives per policy.
- **Acceptance.** `/robots.txt` returns 200 · no critical pages/resources disallowed · sitemap referenced · AI-crawler stance intentional.
- **Tooling.** Search Console robots report/tester, manual fetch, Screaming Frog.
- **Failure modes.** Missing file returning an HTML error page (Armstrong); accidental `Disallow: /`; blocking CSS/JS; no sitemap reference.
- **Verify.** Fetch `/robots.txt` (expect 200); confirm no critical paths/resources are disallowed.

<a id="TE-02"></a>
### TE-02 · XML sitemap
- **Definition.** A machine-readable list of indexable URLs submitted to search engines.
- **Why / engines.** Aids discovery and signals which URLs are canonical/indexable (Google/Bing; helps AI discovery).
- **Implement.** Generate a complete sitemap of canonical, indexable, 200-status URLs only; exclude noindex/redirected/canonicalized/404 URLs; auto-update; submit in Search Console + Bing Webmaster.
- **Acceptance.** Valid XML · only indexable canonical 200 URLs · submitted · auto-updates.
- **Tooling.** CMS sitemap generator, Search Console (sitemap report), Screaming Frog.
- **Failure modes.** Missing/404 sitemap (Armstrong); including noindex/redirected/404 URLs; stale sitemap.
- **Verify.** Confirm the sitemap loads, validates, lists only indexable canonicals; check Search Console for errors.

<a id="TE-03"></a>
### TE-03 · Specialized sitemaps & sitemap index
- **Definition.** Dedicated image/video/news sitemaps and a sitemap index for large sites.
- **Why / engines.** Improves discovery of media and high-volume content; required structure beyond ~50k URLs (Google/Bing; image/video search; AI multimodal).
- **Implement.** Add image/video sitemaps where that content matters; split large sites via a sitemap index (≤50k URLs / ≤50MB per file); keep each scoped and current.
- **Acceptance.** Media sitemaps present where relevant · sitemap index used at scale · files within size limits.
- **Tooling.** CMS/plugin sitemap generators, Screaming Frog.
- **Failure modes.** One oversized sitemap; no image/video sitemaps for media-heavy sites; stale media entries.
- **Verify.** Confirm media content is covered and the index references all child sitemaps.

<a id="TE-04"></a>
### TE-04 · HTML sitemap
- **Definition.** A user-facing page linking to the site's key pages.
- **Why / engines.** Aids navigation and provides a crawl path to deeper pages (Google/Bing; UX).
- **Implement.** Provide an HTML sitemap for larger sites linking to important sections/pages; keep it current and genuinely useful, not a link dump.
- **Acceptance.** HTML sitemap present (for sites large enough to need it) · links to key pages · current.
- **Tooling.** CMS HTML-sitemap module, Screaming Frog (link path check).
- **Failure modes.** Stale HTML sitemap; dumping every URL; treating it as a substitute for good navigation.
- **Verify.** Confirm it links to current key pages and provides a crawl path to deep content.

<a id="TE-05"></a>
### TE-05 · Indexation control
- **Definition.** Managing which pages are indexable via meta robots / noindex and preventing index bloat.
- **Why / engines.** Keeps low-value/duplicate/thin pages out of the index and concentrates quality (Google/Bing).
- **Implement.** Index valuable unique pages; noindex thin/utility/duplicate pages (tag/search/filter results, thank-you pages); audit indexed count vs. intended. Per Allegiant policy, noindex on corpus pages is temporary contamination-mitigation only — restore `index,follow` at decontamination sign-off; never index a still-contaminated page.
- **Acceptance.** Indexed page count ≈ intended count · no index bloat · noindex applied intentionally · no valuable page accidentally noindexed.
- **Tooling.** Search Console (Pages/coverage report), Screaming Frog (indexability), `site:` check.
- **Failure modes.** Index bloat from utility pages; accidentally noindexing money pages; leaving a launch-time site-wide noindex live.
- **Verify.** Compare `site:` count and Search Console indexed count to intended; confirm no money page is noindexed.

<a id="TE-06"></a>
### TE-06 · Canonical tags
- **Definition.** `rel=canonical` declaring the preferred URL among duplicates/variants.
- **Why / engines.** Consolidates signals and prevents duplicate-content dilution (Google/Bing).
- **Implement.** Self-referencing canonical on every indexable page; point true duplicates/parameter variants to the canonical; keep canonical, internal links, and sitemap consistent.
- **Acceptance.** Every indexable page has a canonical · duplicates point to the right canonical · no canonical/sitemap/internal-link conflicts.
- **Tooling.** Screaming Frog (canonical audit), Search Console (page indexing reasons), view-source.
- **Failure modes.** Missing canonicals (Tucker — 4 pages); canonicalizing everything to the homepage; canonical contradicting noindex or sitemap.
- **Verify.** Crawl for missing/conflicting canonicals; confirm duplicates resolve to the intended URL.

<a id="TE-07"></a>
### TE-07 · Crawl-budget optimization
- **Definition.** Ensuring crawlers spend their crawl allocation on valuable URLs (matters most on large sites).
- **Why / engines.** Wasted crawl on low-value/duplicate URLs delays indexing of important pages (Google/Bing).
- **Implement.** Reduce crawl waste — fix parameter/faceted explosion, eliminate redirect chains and soft-404s, prune/noindex low-value URLs, keep the sitemap clean; small sites rarely need active management.
- **Acceptance.** Crawl concentrated on valuable URLs · low crawl waste · important pages crawled regularly.
- **Tooling.** Log-file analysis (TE-39), Search Console (Crawl Stats), Screaming Frog.
- **Failure modes.** Faceted/parameter URL explosion; crawling endless calendar/filter pages; chains burning budget.
- **Verify.** Log analysis: confirm crawlers spend time on money pages, not parameter junk.

<a id="TE-08"></a>
### TE-08 · Crawl-error monitoring
- **Definition.** Tracking 404s, soft-404s, server (5xx) errors, and other crawl issues.
- **Why / engines.** Errors waste crawl, harm UX, and can drop pages from the index (Google/Bing).
- **Implement.** Monitor Search Console coverage; fix or properly 410/301 genuine 404s; resolve soft-404s (thin pages returning 200) and 5xx errors promptly.
- **Acceptance.** No unexpected 404/5xx on important URLs · soft-404s resolved · errors triaged on a cadence.
- **Tooling.** Search Console (Pages report), Screaming Frog, server logs.
- **Failure modes.** Soft-404s returning 200 (Armstrong); ignoring 5xx spikes; broken-link rot.
- **Verify.** Review Search Console coverage; confirm no important URLs throw 404/5xx.

<a id="TE-09"></a>
### TE-09 · Redirect management
- **Definition.** Correct use of 301 (permanent) vs. 302 (temporary), with no chains or loops.
- **Why / engines.** Proper redirects preserve signals on URL changes; chains/loops waste crawl and lose equity (Google/Bing).
- **Implement.** Use 301 for permanent moves; redirect to the final URL directly (no chains); fix loops; update internal links to point at final URLs rather than relying on redirects.
- **Acceptance.** Permanent moves use 301 · no redirect chains/loops · internal links point to final URLs.
- **Tooling.** Screaming Frog (redirect chains report), redirect-checker, Search Console.
- **Failure modes.** 302 used for permanent moves; multi-hop chains; loops; internal links to redirected URLs.
- **Verify.** Crawl for chains/loops; confirm changed URLs 301 directly to their destination.

<a id="TE-10"></a>
### TE-10 · Broken internal link remediation
- **Definition.** Eliminating internal links that resolve to 404/error pages.
- **Why / engines.** Broken internal links waste crawl, harm UX, and signal neglect (Google/Bing; UX).
- **Implement.** Crawl for internal 404s; fix or remove them; update links after URL changes; monitor on a cadence.
- **Acceptance.** Zero internal links to 404/error URLs · fixed after any URL change.
- **Tooling.** Screaming Frog (internal broken links), Search Console.
- **Failure modes.** 404s after migrations (the recurring pattern); 7 broken links live (Tucker); rot from deleted pages.
- **Verify.** Crawl; confirm no internal links resolve to 404/error.

<a id="TE-11"></a>
### TE-11 · Orphan-page detection
- **Definition.** Finding indexable pages with no internal links pointing to them.
- **Why / engines.** Orphans are hard to discover/rank and signal weak architecture (Google/Bing).
- **Implement.** Cross-reference crawl vs. sitemap/analytics/log data to find orphans; link important orphans into the architecture; noindex/remove genuinely unneeded ones.
- **Acceptance.** No important page is orphaned · every money page has internal inlinks (ties ON-20).
- **Tooling.** Screaming Frog (orphan report via sitemap/GA/GSC integration), log files.
- **Failure modes.** Money pages reachable only via sitemap; programmatically-generated orphans; no internal path to deep pages.
- **Verify.** Run an orphan report; confirm priority pages have internal inlinks.

<a id="TE-12"></a>
### TE-12 · Site architecture & crawl depth
- **Definition.** A logical hierarchy keeping important pages within ~3 clicks of the homepage.
- **Why / engines.** Shallow, logical architecture aids crawl, discovery, and authority flow (Google/Bing; helps AI map coverage).
- **Implement.** Design a clear hierarchy (home → category → page); keep money pages shallow; use navigation, hubs, and internal links to flatten depth.
- **Acceptance.** Important pages ≤ ~3 clicks deep · logical hierarchy · no deeply-buried money pages.
- **Tooling.** Screaming Frog (crawl depth/site structure), architecture map.
- **Failure modes.** Money pages buried deep; flat dumps with no hierarchy; only ~8 pages reachable from home (Armstrong).
- **Verify.** Crawl-depth report: confirm priority pages sit within ~3 clicks.

<a id="TE-13"></a>
### TE-13 · URL consistency
- **Definition.** Consistent URL conventions — parameter handling, trailing slashes, casing.
- **Why / engines.** Inconsistent URL variants create duplicate-content and crawl issues (Google/Bing).
- **Implement.** Pick one convention (trailing slash or not, lowercase) and enforce site-wide; 301 variants to the canonical form; avoid unnecessary parameters on indexable URLs.
- **Acceptance.** One consistent URL form · variants 301 to canonical · no case/slash duplicates indexed.

- **Failure modes.** Both `/page` and `/page/` resolving 200; mixed casing; `/index.php/contact` duplicating `/contact` (Armstrong).
- **Verify.** Test slash/case/parameter variants; confirm they 301 to the single canonical form.

<a id="TE-14"></a>
### TE-14 · Faceted navigation & parameter handling
- **Definition.** Controlling crawl/index of filter, sort, and parameter-generated URLs.
- **Why / engines.** Faceted navigation can generate near-infinite duplicate URLs that drain crawl budget (Google/Bing).
- **Implement.** Decide which facets are indexable; noindex/canonicalize/robots-control the rest; avoid linking to infinite filter combinations; keep valuable facet pages, suppress the combinatorial explosion.
- **Acceptance.** No crawlable infinite parameter space · valuable facets indexable, junk facets suppressed · canonical/robots strategy defined.
- **Tooling.** Log analysis, Screaming Frog (parameter crawl), Search Console.
- **Failure modes.** Crawlable infinite filter/sort combinations; canonical chaos across facets; indexing thin filter pages.
- **Verify.** Crawl with parameters; confirm no infinite space and that junk facets are suppressed.

<a id="TE-15"></a>
### TE-15 · Duplicate-content control
- **Definition.** Preventing the same/near-same content from existing at multiple URLs (www/non-www, http/https, parameters, near-dupes).
- **Why / engines.** Duplication splits signals and suppresses rankings; high site-wide duplication actively harms (Google/Bing).
- **Implement.** Enforce one protocol+host (301 the rest); canonicalize parameter dupes; rewrite near-duplicate templated/boilerplate content (ties ON-29); keep duplication well under ~10%.
- **Acceptance.** Single protocol+host · parameter dupes canonicalized · site-wide duplication low · no near-dup templates.
- **Tooling.** Siteliner/Copyscape, Screaming Frog (near-dupe), Search Console.
- **Failure modes.** 38% duplicate content (Tucker); www + non-www both resolving 200; boilerplate-heavy templated pages.
- **Verify.** Run a duplication scan; confirm one host/protocol resolves and duplication is low.

<a id="TE-16"></a>
### TE-16 · HTTPS / SSL
- **Definition.** Serving the whole site over a valid TLS certificate.
- **Why / engines.** A confirmed (if lightweight) ranking signal and a baseline trust/security requirement (Google/Bing; UX/trust; AI trust).
- **Implement.** Valid cert covering all hostnames; force HTTPS site-wide (301 HTTP→HTTPS); auto-renew the cert; add HSTS where appropriate.
- **Acceptance.** Valid cert · all HTTP 301s to HTTPS · no expired/mismatched cert · HSTS considered.
- **Tooling.** SSL checkers (SSL Labs), browser security panel, Screaming Frog.
- **Failure modes.** Expired/mismatched cert; HTTP still reachable; HSTS/security headers absent (Armstrong).
- **Verify.** Load HTTP and confirm it 301s to HTTPS; check cert validity and coverage.

<a id="TE-17"></a>
### TE-17 · Mixed-content resolution
- **Definition.** Ensuring HTTPS pages load no insecure (HTTP) sub-resources.
- **Why / engines.** Mixed content triggers browser warnings and breaks the secure-context trust signal (Google/Bing; UX/trust).
- **Implement.** Serve all assets (images, scripts, styles, fonts) over HTTPS; fix hardcoded HTTP references; use protocol-relative or HTTPS URLs.
- **Acceptance.** Zero mixed-content warnings · all sub-resources load over HTTPS.
- **Tooling.** Browser console (mixed-content warnings), Screaming Frog, SSL checkers.
- **Failure modes.** Hardcoded HTTP image/script URLs; third-party embeds over HTTP; legacy CMS asset paths.
- **Verify.** Open the console on key pages; confirm no mixed-content warnings.

<a id="TE-18"></a>
### TE-18 · Mobile-first indexing readiness
- **Definition.** Ensuring the mobile rendering contains the same content, structured data, and metadata as desktop.
- **Why / engines.** Google indexes the mobile version; content missing on mobile is effectively invisible (Google; mobile-first is universal now).
- **Implement.** Serve full content/links/structured data on mobile (no hiding behind "desktop-only"); verify parity of text, images (with alt), and schema across viewports.
- **Acceptance.** Mobile content = desktop content · structured data + metadata present on mobile · no mobile content gaps.

- **Failure modes.** Content/links hidden on mobile; schema only on desktop; truncated mobile templates.

<a id="TE-19"></a>
### TE-19 · Responsive / mobile-friendly rendering
- **Definition.** Layouts that adapt and remain usable across viewport sizes.
- **Why / engines.** Mobile usability is a page-experience factor and the majority traffic reality (Google; UX).

- **Acceptance.** Usable across viewports · no horizontal scroll · tap targets adequately sized/spaced · text readable.

<a id="TE-20"></a>
### TE-20 · Core Web Vitals: LCP (Largest Contentful Paint)
- **Definition.** Time to render the largest visible content element (loading performance).
- **Why / engines.** A page-experience ranking input and a major UX/conversion factor (Google; UX). Thresholds: good ≤ 2.5s · needs-improvement 2.5–4s · poor > 4s.
- **Implement.** Optimize the LCP element — compress/right-size the hero image (TE-25), reduce TTFB (TE-23), eliminate render-blocking resources (TE-24), preload the LCP asset.
- **Acceptance.** Field LCP ≤ 2.5s (good) at the 75th percentile · LCP element identified and optimized.
- **Tooling.** PageSpeed Insights / CrUX (field), Lighthouse (lab), Search Console (Core Web Vitals report).
- **Failure modes.** Catastrophic LCP (Kirchner 20.6s mobile; Armstrong 5.2s); huge un-optimized hero (157KB+ JPG, no srcset); slow TTFB.
- **Verify.** PageSpeed field data: confirm 75th-percentile LCP is in "good" on mobile + desktop.

<a id="TE-21"></a>
### TE-21 · Core Web Vitals: INP (Interaction to Next Paint)
- **Definition.** Responsiveness — latency from user interaction to the next visual update. **Replaced FID in March 2024.**
- **Why / engines.** A page-experience ranking input measuring real interactivity (Google; UX). Thresholds: good ≤ 200ms · needs-improvement 200–500ms · poor > 500ms.
- **Implement.** Reduce main-thread work; break up long JS tasks; minimize/defer third-party scripts; avoid heavy event handlers; optimize input responsiveness.
- **Acceptance.** Field INP ≤ 200ms (good) at the 75th percentile.
- **Tooling.** PageSpeed/CrUX (field INP), Lighthouse (lab proxies), web-vitals JS, Search Console.
- **Failure modes.** Heavy JS blocking the main thread; bloated third-party tags; legacy form scripts (Armstrong's 25+-field Fabrik form with hidden tokens).
- **Verify.** PageSpeed field data: confirm 75th-percentile INP ≤ 200ms.

<a id="TE-22"></a>
### TE-22 · Core Web Vitals: CLS (Cumulative Layout Shift)
- **Definition.** Visual stability — how much layout shifts unexpectedly during load.
- **Why / engines.** A page-experience ranking input and a UX irritant (Google; UX). Thresholds: good ≤ 0.1 · needs-improvement 0.1–0.25 · poor > 0.25.
- **Implement.** Set explicit width/height (or aspect-ratio) on images/embeds; reserve space for ads/dynamic content; avoid injecting content above existing content; preload fonts to avoid swap shifts.
- **Acceptance.** Field CLS ≤ 0.1 (good) at the 75th percentile.
- **Tooling.** PageSpeed/CrUX, Lighthouse, Search Console.
- **Failure modes.** Images without dimensions; late-loading ads/banners pushing content; font swap shifts.
- **Verify.** PageSpeed field data: confirm 75th-percentile CLS ≤ 0.1.

<a id="TE-23"></a>
### TE-23 · Server response / TTFB
- **Definition.** Time to first byte — server responsiveness before rendering begins.
- **Why / engines.** A slow TTFB caps LCP and overall speed; a key infrastructure lever (Google/Bing; UX). A common mobile-vs-desktop gap.
- **Implement.** Fast hosting; server/page caching; CDN (TE-27); optimized backend/DB queries; reduce origin work per request.
- **Acceptance.** TTFB consistently low (target well under ~0.8s) · no large mobile/desktop TTFB gap.
- **Tooling.** PageSpeed (server response time), WebPageTest, server logs.
- **Failure modes.** 3.8× mobile/desktop TTFB gap (Armstrong: 1.40s vs 0.37s); no caching; slow shared hosting; uncached DB hits.
- **Verify.** Measure TTFB on mobile + desktop; confirm it's low and the gap is small.

<a id="TE-24"></a>
### TE-24 · Render-blocking resource reduction
- **Definition.** Minimizing CSS/JS that blocks the browser from rendering above-the-fold content.
- **Why / engines.** Render-blocking resources delay LCP/FCP and hurt page experience (Google; UX).
- **Implement.** Inline critical CSS; defer/async non-critical JS; load only what's needed above the fold; eliminate unused CSS/JS; reduce synchronous resources.
- **Acceptance.** Minimal render-blocking resources · critical CSS prioritized · non-critical JS deferred/async.
- **Tooling.** Lighthouse (render-blocking audit), PageSpeed, WebPageTest.
- **Failure modes.** 17 synchronous render-blocking resources (Armstrong: 9 CSS + 8 JS); large blocking JS bundles; unused CSS.
- **Verify.** Lighthouse: confirm render-blocking resources are minimized and critical CSS is handled.

<a id="TE-25"></a>
### TE-25 · Image delivery
- **Definition.** Efficient image serving — next-gen formats, responsive sizing, lazy loading.
- **Why / engines.** Images are usually the heaviest assets and a top LCP cause; efficient delivery is core to speed (Google/Bing; UX). Pairs with ON-17.
- **Implement.** Serve WebP/AVIF; provide `srcset`/`sizes` for responsive sizing; `loading="lazy"` for below-fold images (never the LCP image); compress; correct dimensions.
- **Acceptance.** Next-gen formats · `srcset` present · below-fold images lazy-loaded · compressed · no oversized images.
- **Tooling.** Lighthouse, Squoosh/ImageOptim, Screaming Frog (image audit).
- **Failure modes.** 157KB JPG with zero `srcset`, zero `loading="lazy"` across 12 images (Armstrong); full-res image to mobile; lazy-loading the LCP image.
- **Verify.** Audit image weights/formats; confirm srcset + lazy-load (except LCP) on key pages.

<a id="TE-26"></a>
### TE-26 · Minification & compression
- **Definition.** Minifying HTML/CSS/JS and compressing responses (gzip/brotli).
- **Why / engines.** Reduces transfer size and speeds delivery (Google/Bing; UX).
- **Implement.** Minify HTML/CSS/JS; enable brotli (preferred) or gzip at the server/CDN; bundle/tree-shake where it helps.
- **Acceptance.** Text assets minified · compression enabled (brotli/gzip) · no obviously bloated payloads.
- **Tooling.** Lighthouse, WebPageTest, browser network panel (check Content-Encoding).
- **Failure modes.** Uncompressed responses; unminified production assets; no brotli.
- **Verify.** Network panel: confirm `Content-Encoding: br/gzip` and minified assets.

<a id="TE-27"></a>
### TE-27 · Caching & CDN
- **Definition.** Browser caching, server caching, and edge delivery via a CDN.
- **Why / engines.** Cuts repeat-load time and TTFB; improves global performance and resilience (Google/Bing; UX).
- **Implement.** Set sensible cache headers for static assets; use a CDN for static/edge delivery; enable page caching where appropriate; avoid no-store on cacheable assets.
- **Acceptance.** Static assets cached with appropriate headers · CDN serving static content · no `no-store` on cacheable resources.
- **Tooling.** Browser network panel (cache headers), CDN dashboard, WebPageTest.
- **Failure modes.** `no-store/no-cache/must-revalidate` on every page (Armstrong — every request hits origin); no CDN; un-cached static assets.
- **Verify.** Inspect cache headers on static assets; confirm caching + CDN are active.

<a id="TE-28"></a>
### TE-28 · JavaScript SEO / rendering strategy
- **Definition.** How JS-dependent content is rendered for crawlers — client-side (CSR), server-side (SSR), static (SSG), or dynamic rendering.
- **Why / engines.** Google renders JS but with delay/cost; Bing and most AI crawlers render JS poorly or not at all — JS-gated content can be invisible to them (Google/Bing; LLMs).
- **Implement.** Prefer SSR/SSG so primary content is in the initial HTML; if CSR, ensure critical content/links/metadata render server-side or via prerendering; test the rendered DOM crawlers actually receive.
- **Acceptance.** Primary content + links + metadata present in server-rendered HTML · no critical content gated behind client-only JS.
- **Tooling.** Search Console (URL inspection — rendered HTML), "view rendered source," Screaming Frog (JS rendering mode).
- **Failure modes.** Content only in client-rendered DOM (invisible to Bing/AI crawlers); JS-only nav; metadata injected client-side.
- **Verify.** Compare raw HTML vs. rendered HTML; confirm critical content exists pre-JS.

<a id="TE-29"></a>
### TE-29 · Server-side rendering / static HTML (AI readiness)
- **Definition.** Delivering meaningful content in the raw HTML response rather than requiring JS execution.
- **Why / engines.** **Foundational for AI visibility** — LLM/answer-engine crawlers frequently don't execute JS, so content absent from raw HTML can't be retrieved or cited (LLMs; AI Overviews; Bing/Copilot). Core TAR requirement; pairs with AI-15.
- **Implement.** Serve content statically or server-rendered; verify the raw HTML (curl) contains the full content, headings, links, and schema; don't rely on hydration for primary content.
- **Acceptance.** `curl` of the page returns full primary content + schema · no dependence on JS for content AI must read.
- **Tooling.** `curl`/raw-fetch, view-source, Screaming Frog (raw vs. rendered).
- **Failure modes.** SPA shells that return near-empty HTML; content injected post-load; schema added client-side.
- **Verify.** `curl` the URL; confirm the body, headings, and JSON-LD are present in the raw response.

<a id="TE-39"></a>
### TE-39 · Log-file analysis
- **Definition.** Analyzing server logs to see how search and AI bots actually crawl the site.
- **Why / engines.** The ground truth of crawl behavior — what's crawled, how often, what's wasted, and which AI bots are hitting you (Google/Bing; AI crawler visibility). Ties to AI-13/14.
- **Implement.** Periodically analyze logs: crawl frequency of money pages, crawl waste on junk URLs, error patterns, and AI-crawler (GPTBot/ClaudeBot/PerplexityBot/Google-Extended) activity; act on findings.
- **Acceptance.** Logs analyzed on a cadence · money pages crawled regularly · crawl waste identified · AI-crawler activity visible.
- **Tooling.** Screaming Frog Log Analyzer, Splunk/ELK, CDN/server log exports.
- **Failure modes.** Never looking at logs; not segmenting bot traffic; missing that crawlers are stuck on parameter junk or that AI bots are blocked.
- **Verify.** Pull a log sample; confirm important pages are crawled and identify any waste or blocked AI bots.

<a id="TE-40"></a>
### TE-40 · Uptime, DNS & latency reliability
- **Definition.** Reliable hosting, DNS resolution, and consistent low latency.
- **Why / engines.** Downtime and resolution failures harm crawling, rankings, and UX; the apex must resolve (Google/Bing; UX).
- **Implement.** Reliable hosting with monitoring/alerting; ensure both apex and www resolve and one canonical 301s to the other; robust DNS; minimize latency.
- **Acceptance.** High uptime monitored · apex AND www resolve (one canonical) · DNS reliable · latency low.
- **Tooling.** Uptime monitors (UptimeRobot/Pingdom), DNS checkers, status dashboards.
- **Failure modes.** Apex domain doesn't resolve, only www responds (Armstrong); unmonitored downtime; flaky DNS.
- **Verify.** Test apex + www resolution; confirm uptime monitoring is active.

<a id="TE-41"></a>
### TE-41 · Pagination / infinite-scroll crawlability
- **Definition.** Ensuring paginated and infinite-scroll content remains crawlable and discoverable.
- **Why / engines.** Deep items in series/listings must be reachable or they go unindexed (Google/Bing). Coordinates with ON-35.
- **Implement.** Provide real crawlable `<a href>` pagination links; back infinite scroll with paginated URLs; self-canonical each page; don't canonicalize all pages to page 1.
- **Acceptance.** All series items reachable via crawlable links · each paginated page self-canonical · infinite-scroll content has crawlable URLs.
- **Tooling.** Screaming Frog (pagination crawl), Search Console coverage, JS-rendering test.
- **Failure modes.** JS-only "load more" with no URLs; canonicalizing all pages to page 1; deep items unreachable without JS.
- **Verify.** Crawl the paginated set; confirm deep items are indexed and reachable without JS.

<a id="TE-42"></a>
### TE-42 · Site & identity files
- **Definition.** Supporting technical/identity files — favicon, web manifest, and consistent technical identity.
- **Why / engines.** Favicons appear in SERPs (Google) and aid brand recognition; manifest supports PWA/mobile add-to-home; minor but part of a complete, professional technical footprint (Google; UX/brand).
- **Implement.** Provide a proper favicon (per Google's favicon guidelines), a web manifest where relevant, and consistent meta/identity files; ensure the favicon is crawlable and meets size/format requirements.
- **Acceptance.** Valid crawlable favicon (meets Google requirements) · manifest present where relevant · identity files consistent.
- **Tooling.** Favicon checkers, Lighthouse (PWA/manifest), browser inspection.
- **Failure modes.** Missing/non-compliant favicon (no SERP icon); broken manifest; inconsistent identity assets.
- **Verify.** Confirm the favicon renders in SERP/browser tab and meets Google's requirements.

- **Implement.** Logical, shallow menus labeled in the user's language; group by user need, not org chart; keep primary paths obvious; limit choice overload.

- **Tooling.** Tree-testing/card-sorting, analytics (navigation paths), session replay, heatmaps.
- **Failure modes.** Jargon labels; sprawling mega-menus; burying money pages; navigation that mirrors internal structure instead of user intent.
- **Verify.** Hand the nav to someone unfamiliar; can they reach the 3 key destinations without hesitation?

<a id="UX-04"></a>
### UX-04 · On-site search

- **Why / engines.** Lets intent-driven users self-serve; search behavior also reveals demand and content gaps (conversion; insight).
- **Implement.** Provide search on content-heavy sites; ensure relevant results, handle typos/synonyms, no dead-ends; mine search logs for content gaps and zero-result queries.
- **Acceptance.** Search returns relevant results · handles common typos/synonyms · no zero-result dead-ends for real queries · logs reviewed.
- **Tooling.** Site-search analytics, search-log review, the CMS/search provider.
- **Failure modes.** Broken/irrelevant search; ignoring zero-result queries; no search on a large site that needs it.
- **Verify.** Run 5 realistic queries (including a typo); confirm relevant results and useful zero-result handling.

- **Definition.** A visible breadcrumb trail showing where the user is in the hierarchy.
- **Why / engines.** Aids orientation and back-navigation; produces breadcrumb rich results (Google; UX). *Markup: TE-31; on-page: ON-34.*
- **Implement.** Show breadcrumbs on deep pages reflecting true hierarchy; keep labels concise; pair with BreadcrumbList schema.
- **Acceptance.** Breadcrumbs present on deep pages · reflect real hierarchy · accessible · schema validates.
- **Tooling.** Manual review, Rich Results Test, accessibility checker.
- **Failure modes.** Breadcrumbs not matching structure; missing on deep pages; inaccessible.

<a id="UX-06"></a>
### UX-06 · Visual hierarchy & layout
- **Definition.** A layout that guides the eye to what matters in priority order.
- **Why / engines.** Directs attention to value props and CTAs, improving comprehension and conversion (conversion; UX).
- **Implement.** Establish clear hierarchy (size, weight, color, spacing, position); one primary focal point per screen; align layout to the buyer-facing flow (hero → problem → position → proof → plan → CTA); guide toward the primary action.
- **Acceptance.** Clear primary focal point per section · hierarchy guides toward the CTA · scannable at a glance.
- **Tooling.** Heatmaps/attention maps, 5-second tests, design review.
- **Failure modes.** Competing focal points; flat undifferentiated layout; CTA visually lost; decoration over hierarchy.
- **Verify.** 5-second test: after a brief look, can a user state the page's point and the next action?

<a id="UX-07"></a>
### UX-07 · Above-the-fold clarity
- **Definition.** The first viewport immediately communicates what this is, who it's for, and the value.
- **Why / engines.** The fold decides whether users stay; clarity here drives engagement and conversion (conversion; UX). *Answer-first content: ON-11; main-content prominence: ON-25.*
- **Implement.** Lead with a clear value proposition + primary CTA above the fold; no burying the offer under hero fluff or sliders; make the next step obvious without scrolling.
- **Acceptance.** Value prop + primary CTA visible above the fold · purpose clear without scrolling · no offer buried under decoration.
- **Tooling.** Fold preview (multiple viewports), 5-second test, heatmaps.
- **Failure modes.** Generic hero with no value prop; CTA below the fold; auto-rotating sliders that say nothing; content pushed down by chrome.
- **Verify.** Load the fresh page; within the first viewport, is the value and next step clear?

<a id="UX-08"></a>
### UX-08 · Intrusive-interstitial avoidance
- **Definition.** Avoiding pop-ups/overlays that block content, especially on mobile right after arrival.
- **Why / engines.** A confirmed Google penalty for intrusive mobile interstitials, and a direct conversion/experience harm (Google; conversion).
- **Implement.** No content-blocking interstitials on entry; make any modal easily dismissible and non-blocking; respect legally-required notices (cookie/age) which are exempt but should still be minimal.
- **Acceptance.** No content-blocking interstitial on arrival (esp. mobile) · modals easily dismissible · only exempt notices block.
- **Tooling.** Manual mobile testing, Lighthouse, session replay.
- **Failure modes.** Immediate full-screen pop-up on mobile; hard-to-close modals; stacked overlays; newsletter gate before content.
- **Verify.** Load the page fresh on mobile; confirm content isn't blocked by an intrusive overlay.

<a id="UX-09"></a>
### UX-09 · Readability ergonomics
- **Definition.** The physical comfort of reading — font size, contrast, line length, spacing.
- **Why / engines.** Comfortable reading sustains engagement and aids comprehension (conversion; UX; accessibility overlap). *Content formatting: ON-14.*
- **Implement.** Legible body size (≥ ~16px mobile); sufficient contrast (WCAG AA — UX-10); comfortable line length (~50–75 chars) and line height; adequate paragraph spacing.
- **Acceptance.** Body ≥ ~16px on mobile · contrast meets AA · comfortable line length/height · no cramped text.
- **Tooling.** Contrast checkers, readability tools, mobile preview.
- **Failure modes.** Tiny/low-contrast text; overly long lines on desktop; cramped spacing; light-gray-on-white body.
- **Verify.** Read a content page on mobile without zooming or strain; confirm contrast passes AA.

<a id="UX-10"></a>
### UX-10 · Accessibility / WCAG
- **Definition.** Conformance to accessibility standards — alt text, ARIA, keyboard navigation, contrast, focus states.
- **Why / engines.** Serves all users, is an ADA legal requirement, overlaps with technical SEO signals, and clean semantics aid AI parsing (legal; UX; SEO/AI overlap). *Semantic markup: TE-36/ON-27.*
- **Implement.** Target WCAG 2.1/2.2 AA; image alt (ON-17); keyboard operability and visible focus; proper ARIA only where needed; sufficient contrast; accessible forms and labels.
- **Acceptance.** Meets WCAG AA on key templates · keyboard-operable · visible focus · no critical axe violations.
- **Tooling.** axe / WAVE, Lighthouse accessibility, keyboard-only + screen-reader testing.
- **Failure modes.** Missing alt/labels; keyboard traps; no focus indicators; ARIA misuse; contrast failures.
- **Verify.** Run axe (zero critical issues) and navigate a key flow keyboard-only.

### UX-11 · Engagement / dwell signals
- **Definition.** Whether users genuinely engage with the page (scroll, read, interact) rather than immediately leaving.
- **Why / engines.** Evidence (the 2024 Google API leak's Navboost references and DOJ-trial testimony) indicates user-interaction/click signals influence ranking — though Google is cautious about confirming specific metrics like "dwell time." Optimize the *underlying satisfaction*, not the metric (Google — evidence-based, not officially-confirmed; conversion).
- **Implement.** Earn engagement by satisfying intent fast (ON-11), strong content (Part 5), good UX; don't game time-on-page with dark patterns; track genuine engagement to find weak pages.
- **Acceptance.** Money pages show healthy genuine engagement (scroll/interaction) relative to peers · no engagement-gaming dark patterns.
- **Tooling.** GA4 (engagement rate, scroll), heatmaps, session replay.
- **Failure modes.** Chasing vanity dwell metrics; dark patterns to inflate time; ignoring pages users abandon.
- **Verify.** Review genuine engagement (scroll depth/interaction) on money pages vs. site norms.

- **Definition.** Reducing the pattern where users click your result, bounce back to the SERP, and pick a competitor.
- **Why / engines.** "Bouncing back to search" is among the user-satisfaction signals evidence suggests engines weigh (via systems like Navboost); it also signals the page failed the intent (Google — evidence-based; conversion). *Intent match: ON-08.*
- **Implement.** Match the page to the query intent and deliver the answer fast and completely so users don't need to return to search; fix mismatched/thin pages.
- **Acceptance.** Page satisfies the query intent fully · no quick-return pattern on key terms · intent match verified.
- **Tooling.** Search Console (CTR + position vs. performance), analytics (return-to-SERP proxies), SERP intent analysis.
- **Failure modes.** Intent mismatch; thin pages that under-deliver; clickbait titles that disappoint (ties ON-01).
- **Verify.** For key terms, confirm the landing page fully answers intent; investigate any high-impression/low-satisfaction pages.

- **Definition.** Earning more clicks from the impressions you already get — via compelling titles, descriptions, and structured-data presentation.
- **Why / engines.** Higher CTR captures more of existing demand and is itself among the signals evidence suggests engines use (Google; conversion). *Snippet craft: ON-01/02; rich results: TE-33.*
- **Implement.** Write click-worthy titles/descriptions matched to intent; earn rich results (FAQ, breadcrumb, review) to occupy more SERP space; test underperforming high-impression pages.
- **Acceptance.** High-impression/low-CTR pages identified and improved · rich results earned where eligible · titles/descriptions intent-matched.

- **Failure modes.** Ignoring high-impression low-CTR pages; generic titles; missing eligible rich results; clickbait that pogo-sticks.
- **Verify.** Search Console: find high-impression/low-CTR pages; confirm a snippet/rich-result improvement plan.

- **Acceptance.** Ads/chrome don't crowd out main content · no ad-induced layout shift · healthy content-to-chrome ratio.

- **Why / engines.** Broken rendering on a common device/browser silently loses users and conversions (conversion; UX).
- **Implement.** Test across major browsers (Chrome, Safari, Firefox, Edge) and device classes; fix layout/functionality breaks; ensure parity of content and function.
- **Acceptance.** Renders and functions correctly across major browsers/devices · no broken layouts or dead features on common combinations.

- **Failure modes.** Safari-specific breaks; layout breaking on certain viewports; features failing on a common browser.

### UX-22 · Helpful error & empty states
- **Definition.** 404s, errors, and empty states that recover the user rather than dead-ending them.
- **Why / engines.** Good error/empty states retain users who'd otherwise leave and reduce frustration (conversion; UX). *Status codes: TE-37.*
- **Implement.** A helpful 404 (search, popular links, navigation, brand voice) returning a real 404 status; clear error messages with recovery paths; useful empty states (e.g., no search results → suggestions).
- **Acceptance.** 404 page is helpful (search/links/nav) and returns 404 status · errors offer recovery · empty states guide next steps.
- **Tooling.** Manual testing (force a 404/error), status-code check, session replay.
- **Failure modes.** Dead-end 404s; soft-404s returning 200 (TE-37); cryptic errors; blank empty states.
- **Verify.** Hit a 404 and an empty search; confirm helpful recovery paths and correct status code.

### UX-23 · Perceived performance
- **Definition.** How fast the page *feels*, independent of raw metrics — instant feedback, progressive rendering, no frozen waits.
- **Why / engines.** Perceived speed shapes satisfaction and engagement even when raw timings are similar (conversion; UX). *Raw performance: TE-20–27.*
- **Implement.** Immediate interaction feedback; progressive/skeleton loading so content appears incrementally; prioritize above-the-fold rendering; avoid blank-screen waits and janky transitions.
- **Acceptance.** Interactions give instant feedback · content appears progressively · no blank-screen or frozen waits · smooth transitions.
- **Tooling.** WebPageTest (filmstrip), session replay, real-device feel testing.
- **Failure modes.** Blank screen until full load; no loading feedback; janky transitions; perceived freeze on interaction.
- **Verify.** Load + interact on a mid-tier device/throttled network; confirm it *feels* responsive, not frozen.

### EE-02 · Expertise signals

- **Tooling.** Author/reviewer assignment, editorial review, content brief.
- **Failure modes.** Generalist content on expert topics; no visible expertise; surface-level treatment; expertise claimed but not evident.
- **Verify.** Would a subject expert find the content accurate and non-superficial? Confirm qualified authorship/review.

<a id="EE-03"></a>
### EE-03 · Authoritativeness
- **Definition.** Recognition as a go-to source on the topic — by the industry, the web, and within the content's domain.
- **Why / engines.** Authority is built through reputation, citation, and recognition across the web; it's an entity-level signal engines and AI both weigh (Google E-E-A-T; LLMs — entity/brand authority). Ties OF-18/19.
- **Implement.** Build topical authority via consistent depth on a topic, earned mentions/citations, recognized credentials, and being referenced by others; concentrate on owning specific topics rather than spreading thin.
- **Acceptance.** Recognized as an authority on target topics · referenced/cited by others · authority concentrated, not diffuse.
- **Tooling.** Mention/citation monitoring, topical-authority analysis, brand-authority signals (OF).
- **Failure modes.** Thin authority spread across too many topics; no external recognition; claiming authority without evidence.
- **Verify.** Is the brand/author referenced as an authority on the topic externally? Confirm recognition signals.

<a id="EE-04"></a>
### EE-04 · Trustworthiness
- **Definition.** Accuracy, transparency, honesty, and safety — **the most important member of E-E-A-T per Google.**
- **Why / engines.** Trust is the anchor; Google states content lacking trust is low-quality regardless of other strengths, and AI engines avoid citing untrustworthy sources (Google E-E-A-T; LLMs).
- **Implement.** Be accurate (EE-11) and transparent (EE-15); honest claims only (no deception); secure site (HTTPS — TE-16); clear ownership/contact; verifiable everything; no fabrication.
- **Acceptance.** Content is accurate, transparent, and honest · site is secure · ownership/contact clear · nothing unverifiable.
- **Tooling.** Fact-check pass, transparency audit, the correction log.
- **Failure modes.** Inaccuracy; hidden ownership; deceptive claims; insecure site; anything that undermines trust.
- **Verify.** Audit a page for accuracy, transparency, and honesty; confirm no trust-undermining elements.

<a id="EE-05"></a>
### EE-05 · Author bios & credentials
- **Definition.** Real, named authors with relevant bios and qualifications attached to content.
- **Why / engines.** A direct E-E-A-T signal and an entity AI weighs when assessing trust (Google E-E-A-T; LLMs). Ties ON-32.
- **Implement.** Apply the Allegiant byline standard (Chad Markham + Mike Small for SEO/AI SEO — plus the current SEO lead once designated; + Will Holt for SEM); give each author a real bio page with relevant credentials/experience; link bylines to bios.
- **Acceptance.** Substantive content carries a real named author · author has a bio page with relevant credentials · byline links to bio · correct byline per content type.
- **Tooling.** CMS author fields, author-page templates, editorial review.
- **Failure modes.** Anonymous/"admin" bylines; no bio behind the name; wrong byline for content type; generic stock bios.
- **Verify.** Confirm the byline matches the content type and links to a real, credentialed author page.

<a id="EE-06"></a>
### EE-06 · Author as an entity
- **Definition.** Establishing the author as a recognized entity across the web (Person schema + consistent cross-web presence).
- **Why / engines.** Strengthens E-E-A-T and gives AI a coherent author entity to trust and associate with topics (Google E-E-A-T/Knowledge Graph; LLMs). Ties TE-31, OF-23/29.
- **Implement.** Add Person schema (with sameAs to LinkedIn/profiles); keep the author's name, role, and credentials consistent everywhere; build the author's external presence (bylines, interviews, quotes — OF-23/29).
- **Acceptance.** Author has Person schema with sameAs · name/role/credentials consistent across the web · external author presence exists.
- **Tooling.** Person schema validator, Knowledge Graph check, consistency audit.
- **Failure modes.** Author exists only on-site; inconsistent name/role across profiles; no Person schema or sameAs.
- **Verify.** Validate Person schema; confirm consistent author identity across on-site and external profiles.

<a id="EE-07"></a>
### EE-07 · People-first / helpful content
- **Definition.** Content created primarily to help people, not primarily to rank in search.
- **Why / engines.** The core principle behind Google's helpful-content guidance (now folded into core ranking); search-first content is demoted (Google; LLMs favor genuinely useful sources).
- **Implement.** Write to satisfy real user needs first; pass Google's self-assessment questions (does it deliver genuine value, demonstrate first-hand experience, leave the reader satisfied); avoid content made only to capture search traffic.
- **Acceptance.** Content passes the "people-first" self-assessment · delivers genuine value · not created solely for rankings.
- **Tooling.** Google's helpful-content self-assessment questions, editorial review.
- **Failure modes.** Search-first content stuffed with keywords; content with no genuine value; writing for the algorithm over the reader.
- **Verify.** Run the page against Google's helpful-content questions; confirm it would satisfy a real person.

<a id="EE-08"></a>
### EE-08 · Original information & reporting
- **Definition.** Proprietary data, primary research, original analysis, or first-hand reporting — "information gain."
- **Why / engines.** Originality is an explicit quality signal, and original data is among the most-cited material by AI (Google; LLMs). Ties ON-31, OF-10.
- **Implement.** Produce original surveys/data, proprietary insights, first-hand testing, or analysis the SERP lacks; make the page the *source*, not a synthesis of others.
- **Acceptance.** Content contains original information not available elsewhere · adds genuine information gain · positioned as a primary source.
- **Tooling.** Data/survey tools, SERP gap analysis, originality review.
- **Failure modes.** Rehashing existing content; "me-too" pages with no new info; synthesis with nothing original added.
- **Verify.** Identify the specific original contribution; confirm it isn't already on the SERP.

<a id="EE-09"></a>
### EE-09 · Substantial, complete coverage
- **Definition.** Thorough treatment that fully covers the topic for its purpose.
- **Why / engines.** Comprehensive, self-contained content is rewarded by core updates and is the strongest predictor of AI extraction (Google; AI Overviews; LLMs). Ties ON-09.
- **Implement.** Cover the topic completely for its intent — every sub-question, follow-up, and angle a thorough source would; depth proportional to the topic, not padded.
- **Acceptance.** No obvious gap in coverage for the topic's purpose · self-contained · matches/exceeds the best competing coverage · no padding.
- **Tooling.** Content graders, "people also ask," competitor outline analysis.
- **Failure modes.** Thin/incomplete coverage; missing sub-questions; padding to fake substance (ON-30).
- **Verify.** List the topic's sub-questions; confirm the content covers them substantially.

<a id="EE-10"></a>
### EE-10 · Insightful analysis
- **Definition.** Analysis and perspective that go beyond the obvious or easily-found.
- **Why / engines.** Insight differentiates genuine expertise from rehash and is what makes content worth citing (Google quality; LLMs).
- **Implement.** Add genuine analysis — implications, trade-offs, expert judgment, a clearer mental model — not just facts anyone can find; answer "so what" and "why."
- **Acceptance.** Content offers non-obvious insight/analysis · goes beyond restating common knowledge · demonstrates judgment.
- **Tooling.** Editorial review (insight bar), expert input.
- **Failure modes.** Restating the obvious; fact-listing with no analysis; no perspective or judgment.
- **Verify.** Does the content tell an informed reader something they didn't already know or hadn't framed that way?

<a id="EE-11"></a>
### EE-11 · Accuracy & fact-checking
- **Definition.** Verifiable, error-free content — every claim true and current.
- **Why / engines.** Accuracy is foundational to trust (EE-04); errors destroy credibility and risk de-ranking, and AI avoids citing inaccurate sources (Google; LLMs). Core Gauntlet (Pass 2).
- **Implement.** Fact-check every claim against credible current sources; log each correction Before/After/Source; remove or fix anything unverifiable; never publish a stat you can't source; refresh facts on cadence (EE-18).
- **Acceptance.** Every factual claim verified and current · corrections logged Before/After/Source · zero fabricated/unverifiable claims.
- **Tooling.** Source vetting, the correction log, fact-check pass, second-reviewer check.
- **Failure modes.** Unsourced/invented stats; stale facts; "studies show" with no study; errors that erode trust.
- **Verify.** Spot-check claims against sources; confirm the correction log is complete and no claim is unverifiable.

<a id="EE-12"></a>
### EE-12 · Sourcing & evidence
- **Definition.** Supporting claims with citations/links to credible evidence.
- **Why / engines.** Sourcing builds trust and verifiability — what both raters and AI reward (Google E-E-A-T; LLMs value verifiable claims). Ties ON-22.
- **Implement.** Apply the Allegiant outbound-citation rule — every external claim gets a verified outbound link to the authority (credentials → awarding body; regulatory → authority; internal data → internal proof page); link the specific source; verify it's live (broken = Gauntlet fail).
- **Acceptance.** Non-obvious claims sourced · links go to authoritative, live sources · sourcing follows the outbound-citation standard.
- **Tooling.** Source vetting, outbound-link checker, the correction log.
- **Failure modes.** Unsourced claims; links to weak/competitor sources; dead source links; missing authority links on credentials/regulatory claims.
- **Verify.** Confirm each claim has a credible live source link per the outbound-citation rule.

<a id="EE-13"></a>
### EE-13 · Intent satisfaction
- **Definition.** The content actually delivers what the searcher came for.
- **Why / engines.** Satisfying intent is the core of helpful content and reduces return-to-search (Google; AI selects by intent fit). Ties ON-08, UX-12.
- **Implement.** Identify the dominant intent and fully serve it (and the obvious follow-ups) on the page; match format to intent (ON-08); don't make users look elsewhere.
- **Acceptance.** Page fully satisfies the dominant intent + obvious follow-ups · no need to return to search · format matches intent.
- **Tooling.** SERP intent analysis, "people also ask," user testing.
- **Failure modes.** Partial answers; intent mismatch; forcing users to search again for the follow-up.
- **Verify.** After reading, would the searcher's need be met without another search? Confirm yes.

<a id="EE-14"></a>
### EE-14 · YMYL handling
- **Definition.** Elevated rigor for "Your Money or Your Life" topics — health, finance, legal, safety.
- **Why / engines.** Google applies the highest quality/E-E-A-T bar to YMYL because errors can harm people; AI Overviews appear on YMYL but with more scrutiny (Google; LLMs cautious on YMYL). Ties the routing compliance brake.
- **Implement.** For YMYL content, require qualified authorship/expert review (EE-27), rigorous sourcing, accuracy, and required disclaimers; align to the partner's compliance class (medical/legal/financial); never cut corners on YMYL.
- **Acceptance.** YMYL content meets elevated E-E-A-T · expert-reviewed · rigorously sourced · required disclaimers present.
- **Tooling.** Expert review workflow, compliance checklist, QRG YMYL guidance.
- **Failure modes.** Generalist YMYL content; missing expert review/disclaimers; treating YMYL like ordinary content (the routing brake exists to prevent this).
- **Verify.** Confirm YMYL pages are expert-reviewed, sourced, and carry required disclaimers.

<a id="EE-15"></a>
### EE-15 · Site transparency
- **Definition.** Clear disclosure of who's behind the site — about, contact, ownership, policies.
- **Why / engines.** Transparency is a trust signal raters and users check; opacity undermines E-E-A-T (Google E-E-A-T; trust).
- **Implement.** Maintain clear About, Contact, and policy pages; disclose ownership/authorship; provide real contact info and NAP (Austin TX; full street only on NAP-critical surfaces — never Pflugerville); make it easy to learn who's responsible.
- **Acceptance.** About/Contact/policies present and clear · ownership/authorship disclosed · real contact info/NAP · nothing hidden.
- **Tooling.** Transparency audit, manual review.
- **Failure modes.** No/About thin About page; hidden ownership; no real contact info; missing policies.
- **Verify.** Can a visitor quickly learn who runs the site and how to reach them? Confirm transparency pages exist.

<a id="EE-16"></a>
### EE-16 · Editorial standards & review process
- **Definition.** A documented process ensuring content quality, accuracy, and consistency before publish.
- **Why / engines.** Demonstrable editorial standards signal a trustworthy operation and prevent quality drift (Google E-E-A-T; trust). This is the Gauntlet's role.
- **Implement.** Run content through the Gauntlet (P1–P5 with logged evidence; P4 hostile reads never collapsed; Tier-1 = human peer review); document standards; require fact-check and qualified review before publish; never skip the gate.
- **Acceptance.** Content passes the documented editorial process before publish · evidence logged · no content bypasses the gate.
- **Tooling.** Gauntlet SOP + evidence log, editorial workflow, tracker (Complete only after sign-off).
- **Failure modes.** Publishing without review; undocumented/ad-hoc standards; collapsing the P4 reads; skipping the gate under deadline.
- **Verify.** Confirm the page has logged Gauntlet evidence and passed review before publishing.

<a id="EE-17"></a>
### EE-17 · Non-deceptive titles & framing
- **Definition.** Titles, headings, and framing that accurately represent the content (no clickbait/mismatch).
- **Why / engines.** Deceptive titles violate quality guidelines, cause pogo-sticking, and erode trust (Google; UX — ties ON-01, UX-12).
- **Implement.** Make titles/headings honestly reflect the content; deliver what the title promises; no exaggeration, false urgency, or bait-and-switch.
- **Acceptance.** Titles/headings accurately represent content · content delivers on the promise · no clickbait/mismatch.
- **Tooling.** Editorial review, Search Console (CTR vs. engagement mismatch).
- **Failure modes.** Clickbait that disappoints; title/content mismatch; exaggerated claims in headings.
- **Verify.** Does the content fully deliver what the title/heading promises? Confirm no gap.

<a id="EE-18"></a>
### EE-18 · Content-decay management
- **Definition.** Maintaining, updating, pruning, and refreshing content over its lifecycle.
- **Why / engines.** Stale content loses rankings and citations; refreshing often outperforms publishing new, and AI favors current content (Google; LLMs — ties ON-13).
- **Implement.** Schedule reviews; update facts/stats/examples/links; consolidate or prune thin/outdated pages; refresh decaying performers; retire content that no longer serves.
- **Acceptance.** Content reviewed on cadence · decaying pages refreshed · thin/outdated content pruned or consolidated · facts current.
- **Tooling.** Content calendar, analytics (traffic decay), Search Console, content inventory.
- **Failure modes.** Letting cornerstone content decay; hoarding thin/outdated pages; no refresh cadence.
- **Verify.** Audit a sample of older pages; confirm they're current or queued for refresh/prune.

<a id="EE-19"></a>
### EE-19 · Scaled-content-abuse avoidance
- **Definition.** Not mass-producing low-value content to manipulate rankings — **explicitly including AI-generated content** "no matter how it's created" (Google's active March-2024 policy).
- **Why / engines.** A direct spam-policy violation risking demotion/penalty; the central guardrail for any AI-assisted production (Google — enforced through 2026).
- **Implement.** Use AI as a tool *with* genuine human expertise, oversight, originality, and value on top — never mass-generate thin pages; every page must clear the helpful-content + quality bar regardless of how it was drafted; differentiate templated pages (ON-29).
- **Acceptance.** No mass-generated thin/templated pages · AI-assisted content has genuine human expertise/value/oversight · every page clears the quality bar.
- **Tooling.** Quality review, duplication scanner, editorial oversight, the Gauntlet.
- **Failure modes.** Mass AI page generation for rankings; thin templated doorways (ON-29); AI output published without expert oversight/value.
- **Verify.** Confirm each page (however drafted) clears the helpful-content/quality bar and isn't part of scaled thin output.

<a id="EE-20"></a>
### EE-20 · Real-world proof
- **Definition.** Genuine evidence of results and capability — case studies, documented outcomes, real photos, demonstrable proof.
- **Why / engines.** Proof builds trust and experience signals and converts; it's citable and credibility-building (Google E-E-A-T; conversion; LLMs). Must be genuine per integrity standard.
- **Implement.** Use real case studies, documented (anonymized where needed) results, authentic photos, and verifiable proof points; never fabricate or embellish case studies, results, or stats; for hypothetical examples, label them as hypothetical (per the partner-content convention).
- **Acceptance.** Proof elements are genuine and verifiable · hypotheticals clearly labeled · zero fabricated case studies/results.
- **Tooling.** Case-study sourcing, results documentation, the correction log.
- **Failure modes.** Fabricated/embellished case studies or stats (integrity + legal risk); unlabeled hypotheticals presented as real; vague unproven claims.
- **Verify.** Confirm every proof point is genuine and verifiable, and any hypothetical is labeled.

<a id="EE-21"></a>
### EE-21 · Site/creator reputation
- **Definition.** The site's and creator's reputation independent of the site itself (what others say).
- **Why / engines.** External reputation is a key trust input raters assess, and a strong AI-citation driver; reputation abuse (parasite SEO) is a spam policy (Google E-E-A-T; LLMs — ties OF-18/28).
- **Implement.** Build genuine external reputation (reviews, mentions, recognition, independent coverage); monitor and address reputation issues; never engage in site-reputation abuse (hosting third-party content to exploit a domain's authority).
- **Acceptance.** Positive external reputation across independent sources · reputation monitored · no site-reputation-abuse tactics.
- **Tooling.** Reputation/mention monitoring, review platforms, manual reputation review.
- **Failure modes.** Poor/ignored external reputation; below-competitor ratings (Tucker/Armstrong pattern); parasite-SEO arrangements.
- **Verify.** Check independent reputation sources; confirm a positive picture and no reputation-abuse tactics.

<a id="EE-22"></a>
### EE-22 · Differentiated value vs. competitors
- **Definition.** A clear reason this content/source is better than the alternatives ranking/cited for the topic.
- **Why / engines.** Core updates and AI selection reward the best available source; "as good as" rarely wins (Google; LLMs).
- **Implement.** Identify what the SERP/AI answers lack and deliver it — more original data, deeper expertise, better UX, clearer answers; make the page demonstrably the best result, not a tie.
- **Acceptance.** Content offers clear differentiated value vs. the top competing results · demonstrably better, not merely equal.
- **Tooling.** Competitor content analysis, SERP/AI-answer review, content grading.
- **Failure modes.** "Me-too" content; matching but not beating competitors; no clear differentiator.
- **Verify.** Articulate why this beats the current top results; confirm the differentiation is real.

<a id="EE-23"></a>
### EE-23 · Reader-satisfaction completeness
- **Definition.** The reader finishes satisfied and doesn't need to look elsewhere.
- **Why / engines.** The ultimate helpful-content test, tied to satisfaction signals and AI's preference for self-contained answers (Google; AI Overviews; LLMs — ties UX-12, ON-11).
- **Implement.** Answer the question and the obvious follow-ups fully and clearly; lead with the answer (ON-11); leave no critical gap that sends the reader back to search.
- **Acceptance.** Reader's need fully met on-page · obvious follow-ups answered · no critical gap forcing another search.
- **Tooling.** User testing, "people also ask" coverage check, editorial review.
- **Failure modes.** Leaving obvious questions unanswered; incomplete answers; burying the answer (ON-11).
- **Verify.** Would a reader leave satisfied without searching again? Confirm completeness.

<a id="EE-24"></a>
### EE-24 · Brand-authority & recognition signals
- **Definition.** Entity-level credibility — recognized brand, credentials, awards, and authority across the web.
- **Why / engines.** Google increasingly weighs brand/entity authority, and AI familiarity correlates with brand prevalence (Google brand/entity; LLMs — ties OF-18/19). OMNIVIZ EAB.
- **Implement.** Build and surface genuine authority signals — recognized credentials and awards (e.g., the agency's Inc. Power Partner, Google Partner, Semrush/CallRail certifications, BBB A+, 50PROS — or the partner's equivalents), media recognition, branded demand (OF-19); keep claims verifiable with authority links (EE-12).
- **Acceptance.** Genuine authority signals present and surfaced · credentials/awards verifiable · brand recognition growing.
- **Tooling.** Brand-authority signals (OF), credential verification, mention monitoring.
- **Failure modes.** No authority signals surfaced; unverifiable/embellished credentials; ignoring brand-building as "not content."
- **Verify.** Confirm authority signals are present, verifiable, and linked to their issuing authority.

<a id="EE-25"></a>
### EE-25 · Displayed trust assets
- **Definition.** Trust elements shown on-page — reviews, testimonials, certifications, affiliations, guarantees.
- **Why / engines.** Visible trust assets build credibility and convert, and reinforce E-E-A-T (Google E-E-A-T; conversion — ties UX-15, OF-28).
- **Implement.** Display genuine reviews/testimonials, certifications, affiliations, and guarantees near decision points; keep them current and verifiable; never fabricate.
- **Acceptance.** Genuine trust assets displayed near decision points · current and verifiable · no fabricated assets.
- **Tooling.** Review platforms, certification/affiliation records, conversion analytics.
- **Failure modes.** No displayed trust assets; stale/expired certifications shown; fabricated testimonials.
- **Verify.** Confirm displayed trust assets are genuine, current, and verifiable.

<a id="EE-26"></a>
### EE-26 · Entity-fact consistency
- **Definition.** Consistent core facts (NAP, brand name, credentials, key claims) everywhere the entity appears.
- **Why / engines.** Contradictory facts confuse engines and AI and weaken the entity; consistency strengthens the Knowledge Graph and AI's confidence (Google entity/Knowledge Graph; LLMs — ties OF-18, LO-03/10).
- **Implement.** Standardize NAP (Austin TX; full street only on NAP-critical surfaces — never Pflugerville), brand name, and key facts; align on-site, schema, profiles, directories, and mentions; fix contradictions wherever they appear.
- **Acceptance.** Core facts consistent across on-site, schema, profiles, directories · no contradictions · NAP standardized correctly.
- **Tooling.** NAP/citation audit, consistency check across surfaces, schema validator.
- **Failure modes.** Conflicting NAP/phone/founding-date across directories (the Armstrong pattern — 9 NAP inconsistencies); contradictory claims; private address exposed.
- **Verify.** Audit core facts across surfaces; confirm consistency and correct NAP usage.

<a id="EE-27"></a>
### EE-27 · Topic-appropriate expert review
- **Definition.** Subject-matter expert review for content where expertise/accuracy is critical (clinical, legal, financial).
- **Why / engines.** Expert review is expected for YMYL and high-stakes topics and materially raises trust/accuracy (Google E-E-A-T/YMYL; LLMs). Ties EE-14.
- **Implement.** Route high-stakes/YMYL content through a qualified expert reviewer before publish; surface the reviewer (e.g., "medically reviewed by"); document the review; align to the partner's compliance requirements.
- **Acceptance.** High-stakes/YMYL content expert-reviewed before publish · reviewer surfaced where appropriate · review documented.
- **Tooling.** Expert-reviewer workflow, review documentation, compliance checklist.
- **Failure modes.** Publishing YMYL without expert review; no reviewer attribution; skipping review under deadline.
- **Verify.** Confirm high-stakes content carries documented expert review before publishing.

<a id="LO-09"></a>

- **Acceptance.** Prominence signals building (reviews + local links + citations + mentions) · trending up vs. competitors.
- **Tooling.** Local-rank tracking, review/link/citation tools, competitor prominence comparison.
- **Failure modes.** Weak prominence (few reviews/links/citations); the Kirchner pattern (review-qualified but outside the pack — a prominence/other-signal gap); no compounding effort.

## Part 7 · AI SEO / GEO / AEO — Cross-Platform Fundamentals (AI-01 – AI-37)

**Template per item:** Definition · Why it matters (+ engines) · Implement · Acceptance · Tooling · Failure modes · Verify. Each item is tagged with its **OMNIVIZ pillar**: **EAB** (Entity Authority Building) · **ACA** (Answer-First Content Architecture) · **MCN** (Multi-Source Citation Network) · **TAR** (Technical AI Readiness) · **AVM** (AI Visibility Monitoring).
**Conventions:** Binary acceptance checks where possible. **No fabricated stats** — the AI-SEO space is full of vendor claims of uneven reliability; claims here are kept qualitative or evidence-based, never asserted as precise figures.
**Honest framing:** This is the **newest, fastest-moving layer**, and the exact mechanics of each engine are **not public** and **non-deterministic** (the same prompt can yield different sources). Treat these as the **current best-understood practices (mid-2026)**, grounded where evidence is solid and flagged where genuinely uncertain (notably llms.txt adoption). Strong traditional SEO + content quality (Parts 1–6) remains the foundation AI draws from — this layer sits on top, it doesn't replace it.
**Scope:** Cross-platform fundamentals here; **platform-specific tactics (Google AI Overviews, ChatGPT, Perplexity, Gemini, Copilot, Claude) are Part 8.**

<a id="AI-01"></a>
### AI-01 · Answer-first / citation-first structure · [ACA]
- **Definition.** Leading each page and section with the direct answer, structured so an engine can lift it cleanly.
- **Why / engines.** AI engines extract and synthesize; front-loaded, self-contained answers are what get cited (AI Overviews; all LLMs). Ties ON-11.
- **Implement.** Put the direct answer at the top of the page and the top of each section; structure answer → support, not buildup → answer; make answers extractable without surrounding context.
- **Acceptance.** Page + each section lead with a direct extractable answer · answer-then-support structure.
- **Tooling.** Manual extraction test, AI-citation monitoring.
- **Failure modes.** Burying answers under preamble; narrative buildup; answers that need surrounding context.
- **Verify.** Lift the opening of the page/section in isolation — does it answer the query cleanly?

<a id="AI-02"></a>
### AI-02 · Definition-first openings · [ACA]
- **Definition.** Opening a topic with a clear definitional sentence in the form *[Entity] is a [category] that [differentiator]*.
- **Why / engines.** Evidence indicates the first ~150–200 tokens carry disproportionate weight in AI summarization, and definitional openings are retrieved/cited more (AI Overviews; LLMs).
- **Implement.** Lead with a tight definitional sentence; keep the opening paragraph short (≈ under 80 words) so it fits cleanly in an extraction window; state what the thing *is* before elaborating.
- **Acceptance.** Topic opens with a clear definitional sentence · opening paragraph concise (≈ <80 words) · "is a [category] that [differentiator]" pattern where it fits.
- **Tooling.** Manual review, AI-citation monitoring.
- **Failure modes.** Vague/narrative openings; burying the definition; bloated opening paragraphs.
- **Verify.** Read the first paragraph alone — does it clearly define the subject in the first sentence or two?

<a id="AI-03"></a>
### AI-03 · Self-contained passages ("Island Test") · [ACA]
- **Definition.** Each paragraph/block stands on its own and makes sense without the surrounding content.
- **Why / engines.** Engines extract individual passages; a passage that needs context to be understood can't be cited cleanly (AI Overviews; LLMs).
- **Implement.** Write each block as an island — include enough context that it's complete alone; avoid "as mentioned above" / pronoun chains that break standalone meaning; re-state the subject where needed.
- **Acceptance.** Each key passage is comprehensible in isolation · no context-dependent references that break extraction.
- **Tooling.** Manual island test (read each block alone), AI-citation monitoring.
- **Failure modes.** Passages relying on prior context; pronoun/"this"/"above" chains; answers split across blocks.
- **Verify.** Read random key paragraphs out of order — do they each stand alone?

<a id="AI-04"></a>
### AI-04 · Chunk optimization · [ACA]
- **Definition.** Sizing content blocks for clean retrieval — concise, self-contained units per sub-question.
- **Why / engines.** RAG retrieves passages, not whole pages; appropriately-sized self-contained chunks extract better (AI Overviews; LLMs). Evidence points to compact answer units (roughly 40–80-word section openers; self-contained blocks in the low-hundreds of words) and section openers that directly answer the subhead.
- **Implement.** Open each section with a direct ~40–80 word answer to its subhead; keep answer units self-contained; one idea per chunk; avoid sprawling multi-topic blocks.
- **Acceptance.** Sections open with a concise self-contained answer · content organized in discrete extractable chunks · one idea per chunk.
- **Tooling.** Manual review, content structure audit.
- **Failure modes.** Sprawling multi-topic blocks; no direct answer under the subhead; answers spread across paragraphs.
- **Verify.** Check that each section's opener directly answers its subhead in a self-contained way.

<a id="AI-05"></a>
### AI-05 · Semantic completeness · [ACA]
- **Definition.** A passage fully resolves the query/sub-question with no external context or extra click needed.
- **Why / engines.** Evidence indicates this is the single strongest predictor of AI-answer selection — engines prefer passages they can present confidently and completely (AI Overviews; LLMs).
- **Implement.** Ensure each answer is complete in itself; cover the full sub-question, not a fragment; don't make the engine (or reader) go elsewhere to complete the answer.
- **Acceptance.** Each key passage fully answers its sub-question · no missing piece requiring external context · self-sufficient.
- **Tooling.** Manual completeness test, "people also ask" coverage check.
- **Failure modes.** Partial answers; fragments requiring other passages; incomplete sub-question coverage.
- **Verify.** For each sub-question, confirm the passage answers it completely with nothing missing.

<a id="AI-06"></a>
### AI-06 · Question-format headings · [ACA]
- **Definition.** Headings phrased as the natural-language questions users actually ask.
- **Why / engines.** Matches conversational query phrasing and maps content to the sub-questions engines fan out into (AI Overviews; LLMs). Ties ON-05/16.
- **Implement.** Phrase H2/H3s as real questions (from "people also ask," search data, conversational phrasing); place the answer immediately beneath in a self-contained chunk (AI-04).
- **Acceptance.** Key headings phrased as real questions · self-contained answer directly beneath each.
- **Tooling.** "People also ask," Search Console queries, manual review.
- **Failure modes.** Abstract/keyword headings instead of questions; questions with no direct answer beneath; invented questions.
- **Verify.** Confirm headings match real user questions and each has a direct answer below.

<a id="AI-07"></a>
### AI-07 · Fact-dense, specific statements · [ACA]
- **Definition.** Content rich in concrete, specific, verifiable facts — numbers, dates, named specifics.
- **Why / engines.** Engines favor specific, verifiable data over vague assertions when selecting what to cite (AI Overviews; LLMs). Ties ON-31, EE-08.
- **Implement.** Replace vague claims with specifics (concrete figures, dates, named entities); source every stat (EE-12); never fabricate a number to seem specific (integrity standard).
- **Acceptance.** Content is fact-dense and specific · stats sourced · zero fabricated specifics.
- **Tooling.** Editorial review, correction log, AI-citation monitoring.
- **Failure modes.** Vague generalities; unsourced/invented specifics; "many"/"studies show" filler.
- **Verify.** Scan for vague claims to make specific; confirm every specific is sourced and real.

<a id="AI-08"></a>
### AI-08 · Entity optimization & density · [EAB]
- **Definition.** Clearly naming entities (people, places, products, concepts) and making their relationships explicit.
- **Why / engines.** Engines reason over entities; clear entity definition and density help retrieval and association (AI Overviews; LLMs). Ties EE-06, TE-31.
- **Implement.** Name entities explicitly (don't over-rely on pronouns); state relationships clearly ("X, a [type], is part of Y"); reinforce the entities you want to be associated with; mark up with schema (AI-10).
- **Acceptance.** Key entities named explicitly · relationships stated clearly · entity density supports retrieval · schema reinforces entities.
- **Tooling.** Entity/NLP analysis (Google NLP API, content tools), schema validator.
- **Failure modes.** Pronoun-heavy vague text; unclear entity relationships; entities never explicitly named.
- **Verify.** Confirm the page's key entities and their relationships are explicit in the text and schema.

<a id="AI-09"></a>
### AI-09 · Topical authority / consistent association · [EAB]
- **Definition.** Being repeatedly and consistently associated with a topic across your content and the web.
- **Why / engines.** Engines associate brands/authors with topics based on consistent coverage and external signals; depth on a topic builds the association that earns citation (AI Overviews; LLMs). Ties ON-21, EE-03.
- **Implement.** Cover your priority topics deeply and consistently (pillar/cluster — ON-21); reinforce the association via external mentions/coverage on the topic (MCN); be the consistent source for a defined topic rather than diffuse.
- **Acceptance.** Deep consistent coverage of priority topics · external signals reinforce the topic association · concentrated, not diffuse.
- **Tooling.** Topical-authority analysis, AI-citation monitoring (which topics you're cited for).
- **Failure modes.** Shallow coverage spread across topics; no external reinforcement; inconsistent topic focus.
- **Verify.** For each priority topic, confirm deep coverage + external association; check what topics AI cites you for.

<a id="AI-10"></a>
### AI-10 · Machine-readable structured data · [TAR]
- **Definition.** Schema markup that disambiguates content and entities for machines.
- **Why / engines.** Bing/Copilot confirmed LLMs use schema to interpret content; schema aids AI comprehension and entity clarity broadly (LLMs; AI Overviews; Bing/Copilot). Implementation owned by TE-30/31.
- **Implement.** Implement accurate JSON-LD (Organization, Article+Person, FAQPage, Product, Service, etc. — TE-31); relate entities (sameAs, mainEntityOfPage — AI-34); keep markup matching visible content; deploy the Allegiant head stack.
- **Acceptance.** Accurate schema on relevant pages · entities related · matches content · (validation per TE-32).
- **Tooling.** Rich Results Test, Schema validator (see TE-30/31/32).
- **Failure modes.** No/incorrect schema; markup not matching content; no entity relationships.
- **Verify.** Validate schema (TE-32); confirm it disambiguates the page's entities for machines.

<a id="AI-11"></a>
### AI-11 · llms.txt manifest · [TAR]
- **Definition.** An emerging proposed standard — a markdown file at the site root curating/indexing the brand's key content for LLMs.
- **Why / engines.** *Adoption by AI engines is not yet confirmed or universal* — treat as emerging, low-cost, potential-upside, **not a guaranteed factor**. The premise: give LLM consumers a clean, curated content map (LLMs — speculative/emerging).
- **Implement.** Publish a real `/llms.txt` (markdown: brief site description + curated links to your most important content with short descriptions); keep it accurate and current; treat it as belt-and-suspenders alongside solid crawlability (AI-13) and raw-HTML content (AI-15), not a substitute.
- **Acceptance.** Valid current `/llms.txt` present · curates genuinely important content · accurate · maintained.
- **Tooling.** Manual file creation, server-log check for fetches (TE-39).
- **Failure modes.** Treating it as a confirmed ranking lever; stale file pointing to dead/old content; using it instead of real crawlability/SSR; inventing non-standard "directives" (not part of the spec).
- **Verify.** Confirm `/llms.txt` loads, is current, and curates your important content — while not relying on it as a primary lever.

### AI-12 · llms-full.txt · [TAR]

## Part 9 · Measurement & Governance (ME-01 – ME-17)

**Template per item:** Definition · Why it matters (+ engines) · Implement · Acceptance · Tooling · Failure modes · Verify.
